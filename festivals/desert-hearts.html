Before You Go

Desert Hearts is a camping festival ‚Äî there's no hotel-and-shuttle option. Embrace it.

  • Tickets sell fast: The small capacity means this often sells out. Sign up for their mailing list and be ready when tickets drop.
  • Camping gear is essential: Tent, sleeping bag, shade structure, and supplies for 4+ days. This is your home.
  • Bring layers: High elevation Arizona means warm days but cool nights. Pack accordingly.
  • Prepare for altitude: At 7,000 feet, drink extra water and ease into the dancing.

Getting There & Getting In

Playa Ponderosa is near Flagstaff, Arizona ‚Äî a beautiful drive through pine forests.

  • From Phoenix: About 3 hours north. Phoenix Sky Harbor has the best flight options.
  • From Flagstaff: Much closer, but small airport with limited flights.
  • Car camping: Most people drive and camp with their vehicles. Caravans are common and encouraged.
  • Entry: Relaxed and friendly. This isn't a TSA checkpoint ‚Äî it's a welcoming committee.

The Festival Grounds

One stage. One vibe. Zero FOMO. This is the Desert Hearts philosophy.

  • The Stage: A single, well-designed stage that runs 72+ hours nonstop. Every moment is THE moment.
  • Sound System: World-class and dialed in perfectly for the intimate space.
  • The Grounds: Ponderosa pine forests provide natural beauty and shade. Wander and discover.
  • Camp Areas: Set up near friends or meet new ones. The camping IS the festival.

Food, Water & Survival

This is camping ‚Äî come prepared but know there's support.

  • Bring your own: Most veterans bring coolers with food and drinks for the duration.
  • Vendors exist: There are food options available, just don't expect a food court.
  • Water: Bring plenty and use the refill stations. Altitude + dancing = hydration critical.
  • Pace yourself: 72 hours is a marathon. Naps are not just okay ‚Äî they're essential.

The Crowd & Community

This is what Desert Hearts is really about ‚Äî the people.

  • True community: 3,000 people means you'll see familiar faces. By day two, you have a new family.
  • All ages (21+): The crowd ranges from late 20s to 50s. Veterans and newcomers mix freely.
