Before You Go

Imagine is a camping festival‚Äîthere are no hotel options nearby. Plan for full outdoor living.

  • Camping gear: Tent, sleeping bag, air mattress, canopy for shade, camp chairs. Georgia September is still hot.
  • Car camping: Most common option. You park next to your campsite. Bring coolers with ice, food, and drinks.
  • Arrive early Thursday: The pre-party and best camping spots go to early arrivals. Don't show up Saturday.
  • Hydration strategy: Bring multiple gallons of water. Don't rely solely on festival water stations.

Getting There & Getting In

Kingston Downs is about an hour from Atlanta‚Äîrural Georgia at its finest.

  • Drive: The only practical option. ATL airport + rental car, or road trip from anywhere in the Southeast.
  • Entry process: Historically rough‚Äîprepare for long waits in hot sun. Bring snacks and water for the car line.
  • No rideshare: Uber/Lyft won't go out there. Once you're in, you're committed for the weekend.
  • Search policy: Cars are searched on entry. Know what's prohibited before you pack.

The Festival Grounds

The 5,000-acre Kingston Downs property is beautiful but sprawling.

  • Oceanic Stage: Main stage with the biggest headliners and aquatic production elements.
  • Amazonia: The bass music haven. If you're into dubstep and heavy sounds, this is home.
  • Disco Inferno: House and disco vibes, typically open late.
  • Aqua Day Club: Pool area open during daytime. A unique Imagine feature‚Äîactual swimming.

Food, Water & Survival

Camping fest survival requires preparation.

  • Bring your own: Coolers, easy camping food, tons of water. You can cook at your site.
  • Festival vendors: Standard options inside the venue. Lines can be long during peak times.
  • Shade is critical: Georgia September sun is intense. Canopy, hat, sunscreen‚Äîdon't skip any.
  • Electrolytes: You'll sweat more than expected. Pedialyte packets are festival essentials.

The Crowd & Community

Imagine's crowd is its greatest asset.

  • Southeast family: Regulars from Atlanta, Nashville, Charlotte, and beyond. Many return yearly.
  • PLUR culture: Kandi trading, genuine friendliness, helping strangers. It's real here.
  • Campsite community: You'll make friends with neighbors. Group activities, shared supplies, late-night hangs.
  • Age range: Mostly 21-35, with a devoted core of veteran ravers.

After the Festival

The Monday morning exodus is part of the experience.

  • Pack out what you packed in: Leave no trace. Clean your campsite.
  • Don't drive tired: After 3-4 days of festival, you're exhausted. Rest before the drive home.
  • Recovery day: Build in time before returning to work. You'll need it.
  • Stay connected: Exchange info with new friends. The community persists year-round.
