Before You Go

Set expectations correctly. ACL is not an EDM festival with some rock ‚Äî it's a rock festival with some EDM.

  • Study the lineup: Identify the 5-10 electronic acts and plan your schedule around them. They're scattered, not concentrated.
  • Choose your weekend: Same lineup both weekends. Weekend 1 = industry types, Weekend 2 = more casual vibe.
  • Book ACL night shows: Official aftershows at Austin venues extend the music past the 10 PM curfew. Electronic acts often play these.
  • Explore other genres: You're paying for the full lineup. Give that indie band or hip-hop act a chance.

Getting There & Getting In

Zilker Park is central Austin. Access is straightforward but parking is limited.

  • By rideshare: The easiest option. Drop-off zones work well for arrival; departure is chaotic ‚Äî walk a few blocks out.
  • By shuttle: Official shuttles from downtown. Worth considering if you're staying downtown.
  • By bike: Austin is bike-friendly. Many locals bike to Zilker. Bike parking available.
  • Don't drive: Limited parking, expensive, and traffic is brutal. Rideshare or shuttle.

The Festival Grounds

Eight stages spread across Zilker Park. Know where electronic acts tend to play.

  • Honda Stage: Often hosts bigger electronic acts. More production than smaller stages.
  • Miller Lite Stage: Secondary stage that sometimes gets electronic bookings.
  • Smaller stages: DJ sets and emerging electronic artists pop up here.
  • American Express main stage: Headliners ‚Äî occasionally electronic but usually rock/pop.
  • Pro tip: Electronic crowds are smaller at ACL. Less fighting for position than at EDM festivals.

Food, Water & Survival

October weather is forgiving. Food is a highlight. Enjoy yourself.

  • Eat well: ACL food is legitimately good. Budget extra for it. Austin BBQ, tacos, international options.
  • Wine bar: Yes, there's a wine bar. This is not a typical festival.
  • Water: Less desperate than summer festivals. October temps mean you won't die of heat, but hydrate anyway.
  • Barton Springs: Swimming hole adjacent to Zilker Park. Jump in before or after if it's warm enough.

The Crowd & Community

ACL draws Austin's music scene ‚Äî more chill than coastal festival crowds.

  • Locals: Many attendees are Austin locals. More laid-back, less "festival bro" energy.
  • Electronic crowds: Smaller but dedicated. People at the electronic stages want to be there.
  • All-ages: Families, older festivalgoers, diverse crowds. Not purely 18-25 rave culture.
  • Austin weird: The city's "Keep Austin Weird" ethos shows up. Eclectic people, eclectic tastes.

After the Festival

10 PM curfew means the night is young. Austin's nightlife takes over.

  • ACL Night Shows: Official aftershows at local venues. Electronic acts often play intimate club sets.
  • Sixth Street: Live music everywhere. Wander and find something you like.
  • Rainey Street: Bar district with backyard vibes. More chill than Sixth Street chaos.
  • Cedar Street Courtyard: Rooftop vibes, often has DJs during ACL weekend.
  • Kingdom: Austin's electronic music club. Check their ACL weekend programming.
