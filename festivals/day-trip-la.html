Before You Go

This is a city festival ‚Äî pack light and plan simple.

  • Sunscreen is mandatory: June sun on the waterfront is intense. Reapply throughout the day.
  • Comfortable shoes: You'll be dancing on concrete. Support matters.
  • Light layers: Hot during the day, ocean breeze at night. Plan for both.
  • Hotels in Long Beach: Stay close to minimize logistics. The area has good options.

Getting There & Getting In

Long Beach is LA-accessible but not central LA ‚Äî plan accordingly.

  • From LAX: About 30-45 minutes depending on traffic. Rideshare or rental car.
  • From Long Beach Airport: Even closer. Consider flying in here.
  • Rideshare: Works well for getting there. Leaving can be surge-pricey.
  • Entry: Standard Insomniac efficiency. ID and ticket, reasonable security.

The Festival Grounds

The Queen Mary waterfront transformed into a house music playground.

  • Main Stage: Headliners, biggest production, ocean views.
  • Secondary Stages: More intimate vibes, different sounds.
  • The Queen Mary: The historic ship provides a unique backdrop.
  • Ocean Views: Dancing with the Pacific in sight never gets old.

Food, Water & Survival

Standard festival logistics with LA flair.

  • Food vendors: Multiple options but expect festival prices.
  • Craft cocktails: Better than most festivals. Actually decent.
  • Water: Refill stations available. Bring a empty bottle.
  • Heat management: Ocean breeze helps but stay hydrated.

The Crowd & Community

LA house music crowd ‚Äî for better and worse.

  • 21+ policy: Keeps the crowd more mature than all-ages events.
  • Mix of people: House music enthusiasts, LA partygoers, industry types.
  • Vibe varies: Front of stage is committed dancers. Back is more social.
  • Friendly enough: Not the intense community of a camping festival but welcoming.
