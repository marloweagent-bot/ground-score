What Was Air + Style?

Air + Style was Shaun White's unique fusion of professional snowboarding competition and music festival. The Olympic gold medalist brought an event that had previously only existed in Austria and China to Los Angeles, creating something unlike anything else in the American festival landscape.

The concept was audacious: build a massive 16-story snowboard jump in the middle of LA's Expo Park, bring world-class snow athletes to compete, and pair it with a major music lineup. The result was a two-day spectacle that blended action sports culture with festival culture.

Key Details

  • US Run: 2015-2019 (5 years in Los Angeles)
  • Location: Expo Park, Downtown Los Angeles (Rose Bowl first year)
  • Format: 2-day event (Saturday-Sunday in February)
  • Unique feature: Professional snowboard Big Air competition on artificial snow ramp
  • Founder: Shaun White (Olympic snowboarder)
  • Original event: Started in Innsbruck, Austria (1993)

Why It Ended

No official reason was given for the discontinuation. The 2019 edition appears to have been the last, with no events held since. Possible factors include:

  • High production costs (building a snow ramp in Los Angeles is expensive)
  • Competition with other February events
  • Shaun White's shifting focus and eventual retirement from competitive snowboarding (2022)
  • The unique niche made it difficult to scale

The Air + Style Experience

For those who attended, Air + Style offered something genuinely unique:

The Visual Spectacle: Watching professional snowboarders launch 60+ feet in the air against the Los Angeles skyline was surreal. The artificial snow ramp was an engineering marvel ‚Äî snow in February LA.

The Crowd: A mix of action sports enthusiasts, music festival fans, and LA locals created an eclectic atmosphere. Less "rave" than pure EDM festivals, more cross-cultural.

The Sound Issues: The 2015 debut was marred by sound problems ‚Äî Kendrick Lamar famously criticized the sound system mid-set and cut his performance short. Later years improved, but it remained a cautionary tale.

February Timing: Unique positioning in the festival calendar. A mid-winter outdoor event in LA worked because of the mild climate, but limited the audience to those available in February.
