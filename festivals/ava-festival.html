Getting There

  • Belfast City Airport: 15 minutes by taxi to Titanic Quarter.
  • Belfast International: About 40 minutes, bus or taxi.
  • Dublin: 2 hours by train or bus, then local transport.
  • Walking: Titanic Quarter is walkable from central Belfast.

The AVA Conference

AVA includes an industry conference alongside the festival:

  • Talks: Industry panels and keynotes from leading figures.
  • Workshops: Production, DJ skills, and business workshops.
  • Networking: Connect with professionals from across electronic music.
  • Separate ticket: Conference access available as add-on.

Accommodation

No camping ‚Äî you'll need city accommodation:

  • Titanic Quarter: Hotels within walking distance of the venue.
  • City Center: More options, easy transport links.
  • Book early: Festival weekend fills up. Secure accommodation ASAP.
  • Budget options: Hostels available around the Cathedral Quarter.

Survival Tips

  • Afterparties: AVA official afterparties extend the night. Check listings.
  • Layers: Belfast waterfront can be cool in May. Bring a jacket.
  • Cash: Some bars card-only, some cash-preferred. Bring both.
  • Explore Belfast: The city has excellent bars, restaurants, and culture.
