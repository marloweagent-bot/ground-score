Before You Go

Labor Day weekend planning requires some advance work.

  • Book early: Hotels and flights spike for Labor Day. Book accommodation well in advance.
  • Study the map: 7 stages means decisions. Know where your must-see artists are playing.
  • Check the weather: Late August/early September Chicago can be hot, pleasant, or rainy. Prepare for all.
  • Plan your transport: Bridgeview is suburb territory. Rideshare, drive, or check for shuttle options.

Getting There & Getting In

Chicago accessibility makes this manageable.

  • Fly in: O'Hare (ORD) or Midway (MDW). Both have tons of flight options.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft to SeatGeek Stadium works but surge pricing applies post-festival.
  • Driving: Parking available. Chicago traffic is Chicago traffic‚Äîleave early.
  • Entry: Gates open mid-afternoon. Sunset sets are some of the best‚Äîdon't arrive too late.

The Festival Grounds

SeatGeek Stadium transforms into a multi-stage wonderland.

  • The Stadium: Main stage with biggest headliners and production.
  • The Shipyard: Bass haven. If you want heavy, this is home.
  • The Vega: House and tech-leaning bookings.
  • The Chill Dome: Air-conditioned oasis with lasers, cushions, and trippy vibes. Essential for reset.
  • The FirePit: Geometric domes and literal fire. BUNT.'s set here was legendary.
  • Silent Disco: Three channels, choose your adventure.

Food, Water & Survival

Three days requires pacing and preparation.

  • Hydration: Water stations available. Bring a CamelBak or refillable bottle.
  • Food breaks: The NC Yacht Club Boat has a secret bar below deck. Hunt for hidden gems.
  • Chill Dome recovery: Air conditioning, cushions, and trippy visuals. Use it when you need a reset.
  • Comfortable shoes: 7 stages over 3 days = lots of walking. Prioritize comfort.

The Crowd & Community

Chicago's electronic community is passionate.

  • Midwest friendly: Welcoming without being overwhelming. Chicago ravers are good people.
  • Diverse tastes: 7 stages attract fans from every subgenre. Find your people.
  • Creative expression: Paint stations and kandi-making encourage participation.
  • End-of-summer energy: Everyone squeezing out one more festival before fall hits different.

After the Festival

Chicago has plenty to offer.

  • Afterparties: Chicago clubs host official and unofficial after-events throughout the weekend.
  • Chicago food: Deep dish, hot dogs, incredible restaurant scene. Eat well.
  • City exploration: If you have extra time, Chicago's architecture, museums, and neighborhoods are worth exploring.
  • Recovery Monday: Labor Day gives you a built-in recovery day. Use it wisely.
