History

Backwoods started as a boutique Oklahoma event in 2008, grew through various iterations, and found its groove as a crossover festival bridging electronic and jam scenes.

  • 2008-2013: Early years in Oklahoma, building regional following
  • 2014: Rebranded, moved to Tatanka Ranch on Route 66 in Stroud, Oklahoma
  • 2017: Took a break year to restructure
  • 2018: Moved to Mulberry Mountain, Arkansas (former Wakarusa site)
  • 2019: Final full edition with headliners including Zeds Dead, REZZ, Umphrey's McGee
  • 2024: Attempted return cancelled weeks before event date

What Made It Special

Backwoods occupied a unique niche ‚Äî festivals usually commit to one scene, but Backwoods intentionally bridged electronic and acoustic.

"Where else could you see Illenium one night and Yonder Mountain String Band the next? Backwoods was for people who didn't want to choose." ‚Äî Festival veteran, r/festivals 2019
  • Crossover booking: Bass, bluegrass, jam, and eclectic acts sharing stages
  • Mountain setting: Mulberry Mountain was genuinely beautiful
  • Regional accessibility: Served Midwest fans who couldn't travel to coastal fests
  • Labor Day timing: End-of-summer celebration before fall

The 2024 Cancellation

Backwoods announced a 2024 return with a strong lineup including Illenium, CloZee, and Thievery Corporation. Weeks before the event, organizers cancelled citing unspecified circumstances.

  • Refunds: Eventually processed for ticket holders
  • Future: No official announcement about 2025 or beyond
  • Speculation: Financial pressures, venue issues, and the challenging festival landscape post-pandemic

Legacy & Alternatives

For fans mourning Backwoods, similar crossover energy can be found at:

  • Resonance: Ohio-based festival with similar jam/electronic crossover
  • Hulaween: Florida jam fest with electronic integration
  • Secret Dreams: Legend Valley's bass/jam fusion (see our page)
  • Arise: Colorado festival with eclectic booking (when active)
