Before You Go

EDC is a marathon, not a sprint. Three nights of 10+ hour sessions requires preparation.

  • Book early: Hotels near the Strip fill up fast. Shuttles sell out. Don't wait.
  • Camp EDC vs Hotels: Camp EDC eliminates shuttle stress but adds camping logistics. Hotels give you a real bed but mean late-night transport.
  • Physical prep: You'll walk 10+ miles per night. Start breaking in those shoes now.
  • Heat prep: May in Vegas hits 100¬∞F+ during the day. Hydrate before you arrive.

Getting There & Getting In

Las Vegas is one of the easiest festival cities to access ‚Äî cheap flights from everywhere, tons of hotel inventory. The speedway is about 20 miles from the Strip.

  • Shuttles: Official shuttles from Strip hotels. Expect waits of 30-90 minutes, especially post-festival.
  • Rideshare: Works but surges hard. Post-festival Ubers can be $100+.
  • Driving + Premier Parking: Fastest exit but costs $$$. Worth it if you have a group.
  • Entry: Metal detectors and bag checks. Lines vary by entrance ‚Äî research which gates are fastest.

The Festival Grounds

The Las Vegas Motor Speedway becomes a neon wonderland. Eight stages, each with a distinct vibe:

  • kineticFIELD: The main stage. Massive, animated, and where headliners play. Gets crowded.
  • circuitGROUNDS: Second biggest. More diverse bookings, often better crowd flow.
  • bassPOD / wasteLAND: Where the bass heads live. Dubstep, riddim, heavy stuff.
  • neonGARDEN: House and techno haven. More intimate, great for sunrise sets.
  • cosmicMEADOW: Chill grass area with a mix of genres. Good for reset moments.

Food, Water & Survival

You're dancing in the desert for 10 hours. Plan accordingly.

  • Water: Free water stations exist but can have lines. Bring an empty CamelBak or hydration pack.
  • Food: Solid variety ‚Äî pizza, tacos, acai, everything. Prices are Vegas-level ($15-20 entrees).
  • Pro tip: Eat before peak hours (10PM-2AM). Lines are shorter early and late.
  • Electrolytes: Bring powder packets. You'll sweat more than you think.

The Crowd & Community

170,000 people sounds intimidating, but EDC's crowd is generally friendly and welcoming.

  • PLUR is real: Kandi trading, random compliments, helping strangers ‚Äî it happens.
  • Bass stages have the best vibes: Smaller, tighter communities. More headbangers than posers.
  • Main stage can feel impersonal: If you want intimate energy, explore the smaller stages.

After the Festival

Recovery is part of the experience.

  • Don't book a 6AM flight: You'll regret it. Build in a recovery day.
  • Pool parties: Vegas pool clubs go hard during EDC week. Pace yourself.
  • Sleep: The hotel blackout curtains exist for a reason. Use them.
