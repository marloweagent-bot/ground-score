Before You Go

The format changed significantly in 2024. Know what you're getting into.

  • Check if it's happening: No 2025 edition confirmed as of late 2024. Rolling Stone says they want to continue but haven't announced dates. Follow official channels.
  • Downtown hotels: Skip the Strip ‚Äî stay in DTLV for walkability. The D, Golden Nugget, or Fremont hotels put you close to the action.
  • September heat: Still hot but evenings cool down. Starts at 5PM so you avoid the worst of it.
  • It's 2 days now: Not the 3-day fest of old. Plan a shorter trip or add Vegas activities.

Getting There & Getting In

Vegas logistics are easy ‚Äî this festival adds no complexity.

  • Fly into LAS: ~15 min to downtown. Cheap flights from everywhere.
  • Rideshare to downtown: $15-25 from airport. No surge issues getting there.
  • Parking 2024 issues: Organizers pushed $50 valet hard despite cheap city garages nearby. Do your research ‚Äî cheaper options exist.
  • Walking distance: If you stay downtown, you can walk to/from the festival. No transport stress.

The Festival Grounds

Streets become the venue. It's a unique format.

  • Block party layout: City blocks closed off, stages set up in the street. Very different from traditional festival grounds.
  • Arts District location: The 2024 edition moved to the Arts District from old Fremont East footprint. More murals, galleries, local character.
  • Intimate scale: You can actually see the artists. No massive crowd surges or fights for position.
  • Urban backdrop: Graffiti, neon, Vegas grit. Not manicured but authentic.

Food, Water & Survival

An area for improvement in the new format.

  • Festival food was mid: 2024 feedback suggested better local restaurant integration needed. Generic vendors dominated.
  • DTLV restaurants: Amazing food exists blocks away ‚Äî eat before/after. Esther's Kitchen, Carson Kitchen, Jammyland are close.
  • Bar scene: Vegas bars stay open late. Atomic Liquors, Velveteen Rabbit, Herbs & Rye are walkable.
  • Water: September Vegas is hot. Hydrate before and bring a bottle.

The Crowd & Community

The format shift attracted a different audience.

  • Music-focused: People came for Justice and LCD Soundsystem, not for "Vegas party." More knowledgeable crowd.
  • 21+ by default: It's Vegas. No teen energy.
  • Industry presence: Rolling Stone ownership means music industry folks attend. Networking happens.
  • Local support: DTLV community has mixed feelings about festival but generally welcoming.

After the Festival

It's Vegas ‚Äî the party doesn't stop.

  • DTLV bars: Everything stays open. Walk off the grounds and into local bars.
  • Afterparties: Check local venues ‚Äî when big acts are in town, aftershows happen.
  • Late-night food: Tacos El Gordo, Roberto's, or proper restaurants that stay open.
  • Next-day recovery: Vegas brunch culture is strong. Partake.
