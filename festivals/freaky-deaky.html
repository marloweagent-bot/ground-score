Before You Go

Your costume is not optional‚Äîit's essential to the experience.

  • Plan your costume: Go big or go home. Groups coordinate themes. Comfort matters‚Äîyou'll be dancing for hours.
  • Book hotels early: Halloween weekend fills up. Downtown Houston or near the venue both work.
  • Check the weather: Late October Houston can still be warm. Plan for layers and breathable costume options.
  • Pre-game responsibly: It's Halloween weekend‚Äîpace yourself for multiple nights.

Getting There & Getting In

Sam Houston Race Park is northwest of downtown Houston.

  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft work well. Surge pricing kicks in post-festival but isn't brutal.
  • Driving: Parking available on-site. Houston traffic is what it is‚Äîleave early.
  • Entry: Costume inspection can slow things down. Avoid props that look like weapons.
  • No camping: This is a hotel festival. Plan your transport for each night.

The Festival Grounds

The racetrack transforms into a Halloween wonderland after dark.

  • Main Stage: Biggest headliners, full production, massive crowd energy.
  • Bass Stage: Where the heavy stuff lives. Excision fans, this is home.
  • House/Techno Stage: For when you need to groove instead of headbang.
  • Haunted areas: Roaming performers, themed installations, photo ops throughout.

Food, Water & Survival

Standard festival logistics with Texas flair.

  • Hydration: Late October is more forgiving than summer, but still Texas. Drink water.
  • Food options: Festival vendors plus Texas BBQ options. Prices are festival-typical.
  • Costume considerations: Heavy costumes get hot. Elaborate headpieces block views. Plan for comfort.
  • Secure your stuff: Small bag or fanny pack recommended. Don't lose costume pieces.

The Crowd & Community

Texas ravers in full Halloween mode.

  • Costume culture: People put serious effort in. Expect creative, elaborate, and sometimes barely-there costumes.
  • Texas energy: The Houston/Dallas/Austin rave community is friendly and enthusiastic.
  • Age range: Mostly 21-30, with strong 18-20 presence. It's a party crowd.
  • Group vibes: Lots of friend groups and crews. Solo attendees get absorbed into the Halloween chaos.

After the Festival

The Halloween weekend continues.

  • Afterparties: Houston clubs capitalize on the weekend. Check local listings.
  • Late-night food: Houston has excellent late-night food options. Tacos are calling.
  • Recovery: Sunday/Monday you'll be exhausted and possibly still in costume pieces.
  • Return your rental: Check for glitter and costume debris before returning that car.
