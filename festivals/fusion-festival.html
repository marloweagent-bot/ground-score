Getting Tickets

This is the hardest part. There is no way to buy tickets normally.

  • Lottery system: Register on fusion-festival.de when applications open (usually January).
  • Invite codes: Previous attendees can invite friends. Network matters.
  • Volunteer: Work crews can earn access. Commitment required.
  • No resale: Tickets are personalized. Scalping doesn't work.
  • Patience: Some people try for 10+ years before getting in.

What to Expect

  • No lineup: Artists aren't announced. Discover by wandering.
  • No cash: Most things are free or donation-based inside.
  • Participation: Everyone contributes. Bring something to share.
  • Art everywhere: Stages are art installations. The whole site is the exhibit.
  • Lakes: Multiple swimming spots. Bring swimwear.
"Fusion broke my brain in the best way. I thought I understood festivals. I didn't. This is what happens when 70,000 creative people build a temporary city together. Nothing prepares you." ‚Äî Ground Score Research, 2024

What to Bring

  • Bicycle: Essential. The site is huge. Bring lights.
  • Quality tent: Rain happens. Good gear matters.
  • Food to share: Cook for neighbors. Community is everything.
  • Costumes/art supplies: Express yourself. Contribute.
  • Open mind: The most important thing to pack.

Getting There

  • From Berlin: 2-3 hours by car. No direct transit.
  • Carpooling: Fusion forums organize rides. This is the way.
  • Arrive early: Lines on opening day are legendary.
  • Leave late: The exodus is also chaos. Plan accordingly.
