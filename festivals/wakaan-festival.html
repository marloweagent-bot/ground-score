Before You Go

Wakaan is a camping festival on a mountain. Preparation matters.

  • Shade is essential: October in Arkansas is still hot during the day (85-90¬∞F). Bring a canopy with tapestries ‚Äî the veteran move.
  • Layers for night: Temperatures drop significantly after dark. A hoodie and blanket are clutch.
  • Bring your own pizza: There's literally no pizza vendor. It's become a running joke. Plan accordingly.
  • Book early: This festival sells out and grows each year. Don't sleep on tickets.

Getting There & Getting In

Ozark, Arkansas is remote. Most people drive.

  • Airports: XNA (Northwest Arkansas Regional) is closest at ~45 minutes. Tulsa is 2.5 hours.
  • Drive time: ~3 hours from Oklahoma City, ~5 hours from Dallas, ~6 hours from Kansas City.
  • Mountain roads: Take it slow at night. The last stretch is winding.
  • Entry: Expect searches but security is reasonable. The vibe is welcoming, not aggressive.

The Festival Grounds

Mulberry Mountain is the former home of Wakarusa ‚Äî a proven festival venue with great bones.

  • Main Stage: The centerpiece for headliners and Liquid Stranger's sets. Good sightlines from most positions.
  • Second Stage: More intimate, often featuring deeper cuts and late-night weirdness.
  • Layout: Compact and walkable. You won't spend your festival hiking between stages.
  • Camping: First-come, first-served. Arrive early Thursday for the best spots with shade.

Food, Water & Survival

Standard camping festival logistics with a few quirks.

  • Water: Bring a CamelBak. Fill stations are available but stay hydrated in the Arkansas humidity.
  • Food: Decent variety but NOT pizza (seriously, bring your own if you need it).
  • Heat: Don't underestimate daytime temps. Rest during peak heat hours.
  • Camp cooking: Allowed with proper setups. Many people bring grills and coolers.

The Crowd & Community

This is Wakaan's secret weapon. The community is genuinely special.

  • The Wakaan Family: Not just marketing. People look out for each other.
  • Friendliest in bass: Often compared favorably to the Anjuna community for welcoming vibes.
  • No judgement: Weird is celebrated. Be yourself fully.
  • Camp connections: Some of the best memories happen after the stages close.

After the Festival

Post-Wakaan blues are real.

  • Pack out slowly: Don't rush Monday morning. Enjoy the comedown with your camp neighbors.
  • Follow the artists: Many Wakaan roster acts tour together. Keep the vibes going.
  • Join the subreddit: r/Wakaan stays active year-round with the community.
  • Start planning next year: Early bird tickets drop soon after the festival ends.
