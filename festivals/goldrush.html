Before You Go

Desert festival prep is essential.

  • Heat strategy: September Phoenix is still HOT during the day. Lightweight clothing, sunscreen, hat, hydration pack.
  • Western wear optional: Cowboy hats and bandanas are encouraged but not required. The theme is fun, not mandatory.
  • Book hotels: Phoenix metro has tons of options at reasonable prices. No need to stay right next to the venue.
  • Check the lineup: Goldrush curates house-heavy. Know what you're getting into (it's good!).

Getting There & Getting In

Phoenix is accessible and the venue is straightforward.

  • Fly in: Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHX) has great connections. Flights are often cheap.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft work well to/from the venue. Surge isn't brutal.
  • Driving: Parking available on-site. Phoenix traffic is manageable by big city standards.
  • Entry: Standard security, generally smooth process. Arrive before sunset for the full experience.

The Festival Grounds

Rawhide Western Town provides unique atmosphere.

  • Main Stage: Headliners and biggest production. The desert sunset backdrop is stunning.
  • Secondary Stages: House haven, bass music, and genre-specific areas.
  • Western Town: The venue itself has character‚Äîold west buildings, photo ops, themed areas.
  • Layout: Compact and walkable. You won't lose hours walking between stages.
"Goldrush: A Return To The West surprised me in numerous ways. The switch back to Rawhide was refreshing. The 2025 running was exceptionally awesome." ‚Äî Relentless Beats Review

Food, Water & Survival

Desert survival basics apply.

  • Hydration is critical: Bring a CamelBak. Use the water stations frequently. Don't wait until you're thirsty.
  • Electrolytes: You'll sweat more than you realize. Bring supplements.
  • Food options: Standard festival fare with southwestern options. Eat before peak heat.
  • Cool down: Find shade, pace yourself, take breaks. The desert doesn't forgive stubbornness.

The Crowd & Community

Arizona ravers are underrated.

  • Friendly crowd: The Southwest scene is welcoming without being overwhelming.
  • Age range: Mostly 21-30, good mix of seasoned ravers and newer fans.
  • House heads: The lineup attracts people who actually love the music, not just the scene.
  • Western spirit: People embrace the theme with creativity and humor.

After the Festival

Phoenix has more to offer.

  • Late-night food: Phoenix has excellent Mexican food. Find tacos.
  • Pool party recovery: Many hotels have pools. Use them.
  • Desert exploration: If you have time, Arizona's natural beauty is worth exploring.
  • Afterparties: Check local Phoenix venues for official and unofficial after-events.
