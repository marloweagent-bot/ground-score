Before You Go

Forbidden Kingdom requires serious heat preparation. Florida summer is no joke.

  • Hydration strategy: Start hydrating DAYS before the festival. Drink electrolytes. This is survival 101.
  • Light, breathable clothing: Minimal coverage, light colors. Heavy costumes will destroy you.
  • Portable fan: Bring a battery-powered handheld fan. You'll thank yourself.
  • Sunscreen: SPF 50+. Reapply constantly. You will burn without it.
  • Camping vs hotel: Camping is available. Hotels offer AC for recovery. Choose your battle.

Getting There & Getting In

Orlando's infrastructure makes travel easy. The venue has had some parking hiccups.

  • Fly into MCO: Orlando International is one of the busiest in the US. Cheap flights from everywhere.
  • Parking issues: 2024 had complaints about parking organization and off-site parking at extra cost. Consider rideshare.
  • Uber/Lyft: Works well in Orlando. Set pickup points away from main gates to avoid chaos.
  • Security: Day 1 2024 was rigorous (long waits in heat). Day 2 was faster but less thorough. Arrive early.
  • Will call: Get tickets in advance. Avoid day-of will call lines in the heat.

The Festival Grounds

Central Florida Fairgrounds transforms into a medieval bass kingdom.

  • Megastructure: Covered main stage area. Provides shade but can get sauna-like without proper ventilation.
  • The Village: Secondary stage with built-in fans. More comfortable but had sound issues in 2024.
  • Outdoor stages: Full sun exposure. Plan your schedule around shade availability.
  • Sound system: 2024 had some technical issues during headliners (speakers blowing during Subtronics, BTSM). Usually recovered quickly.

Food, Water & Survival

This section is called survival for a reason. Heat management is everything.

  • Water stations: Free water available but lines get long. Bring a CamelBak and fill up constantly.
  • Misting stations: Located in the center of the festival. Use them. Frequently.
  • Electrolytes: Water alone won't cut it. Bring powder packets or buy sports drinks inside.
  • Eat early: Get food before temperatures peak. Eating in 95¬∞F heat is miserable.
  • Know the signs: Dizziness, nausea, confusion = heat exhaustion. Find medical immediately. This is serious.
  • Buddy system: Watch your crew. Heat stroke can sneak up on people. Look out for each other.

The Crowd & Community

Bass music attracts a devoted, specific community.

  • Headbanger tribe: These are your people if you love dubstep and riddim. Devoted fans who know the music.
  • Heat affects mood: 2024 saw some crowd irritability due to extreme heat. Most people stay positive but the conditions test patience.
  • PLUR is present: Despite the heavy music, the community is welcoming. Kandi trading happens.
  • Young crowd: 18+ means college-age dominates. Energy is high but so is occasional chaos.
  • The pits: Mosh pits happen at bass festivals. Know where they form and avoid if that's not your thing.

After the Festival

Orlando has plenty of recovery options.

  • Theme parks: It's Orlando ‚Äî Disney, Universal, and more if you have energy left.
  • Hotel pools: Recovery in water. Most Orlando hotels have pools. Use them.
  • After-parties: Check local venues for official and unofficial after-hours. The bass doesn't stop at the gate.
  • AC recovery: Seriously, just lie in air conditioning. Your body needs it after two days in that heat.
