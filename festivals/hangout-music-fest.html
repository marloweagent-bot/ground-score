Before You Go

Hangout is a beach vacation that happens to have great music. Plan accordingly.

  • Book beachfront: Hotels/condos on the beach are walkable to the venue. Worth the premium.
  • Study the electronic acts: They're spread across stages. Know where Boom Boom Tent is, plan your route to catch electronic acts on main stages.
  • Pack for beach + festival: Swimsuit by day, festival outfit by night. Sunscreen is essential.
  • Hydrate before you arrive: May in Alabama is hot. Start hydrating days before.

Getting There & Getting In

Gulf Shores requires some planning, but it's manageable.

  • Fly into Pensacola: PNS is ~45 min drive. More flight options than Mobile.
  • Mobile airport: MOB is ~1.5 hours. Sometimes cheaper flights.
  • Driving: Most attendees drive from regional cities. Easy 4-8 hour drives from Atlanta, Nashville, New Orleans, Houston.
  • Walk from your hotel: Beachfront properties mean no transport needed. This is the move.

The Festival Grounds

The beach IS the grounds. Stages on the sand.

  • Hangout Stage: Main stage at one end of the beach. Headliners play here. Good sightlines from the sand.
  • Surf Stage: Other end of the beach. Second-tier acts. Scenic ocean backdrop.
  • Boom Boom Tent: The electronic home base. Indoor tent with club-style production. This is where you'll spend most time if you're chasing electronic acts.
  • Beach between stages: Walking in sand takes time. Budget for it. Or make it part of the experience.
"Nothing beats catching Subtronics in the Boom Boom Tent and then walking out onto the beach with the Gulf right there. The contrast is wild." ‚Äî Reddit Hangout veteran, 2024

Food, Water & Survival

Beach festival survival is different.

  • Sunscreen is non-negotiable: You will burn. Reapply constantly.
  • Hydrate aggressively: Heat + dancing + alcohol = danger zone. Water stations available.
  • Festival food is decent: Seafood emphasis makes sense given location. Typical festival prices.
  • Walkable hotels: If you're close enough, you can leave for real food and return. Some people do lunch at their condo.

The Crowd & Community

Beach festival brings a specific energy.

  • Vacation mode: People are relaxed. It's a beach trip. Less intense than dedicated EDM festivals.
  • Mixed genres: You'll share the beach with country fans, rock fans, everyone. The electronic stages have the dance music heads.
  • 18+ means young: College crowds from the South are well-represented. Expect that energy.
  • Friendly vibes: Beach setting keeps everyone chill. Hard to be angry when you're on vacation.

After the Festival

Gulf Shores is a beach town. Lean into it.

  • Late-night beach walks: The beach doesn't close. Post-festival beach time is peaceful.
  • Hotel/condo parties: The real afterparties. Crews rent beach houses and continue the party.
  • Next-day recovery: Sleep in, hit the beach, prepare for another night. It's a vacation rhythm.
  • Seafood crawls: Gulf Shores has excellent fresh seafood. Eat well between days.
