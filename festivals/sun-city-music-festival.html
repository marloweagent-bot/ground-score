What Was Sun City Music Festival?

Sun City Music Festival (SCMF) was the Southwest's largest electronic music festival, held annually over Labor Day weekend in El Paso, Texas. For seven years (2011-2017), it brought world-class EDM to the border region, establishing El Paso as an unexpected but beloved destination on the festival circuit.

Founded by SMG Events and Disco Donnie Presents, SCMF earned a reputation for punching above its weight ‚Äî booking headliners like Ti√´sto, Armin van Buuren, and Steve Aoki while maintaining an intimate, community-driven atmosphere that larger festivals often lack.

Key Details

  • Active Years: 2011-2017 (7 editions)
  • Location: Ascarate Park, El Paso, TX (Cohen Stadium in 2011)
  • When: Labor Day Weekend (Saturday-Sunday)
  • Stages: 3 stages covering all EDM genres, including a Beatport Stage
  • Founders: SMG Events + Disco Donnie Presents
  • Attendance: Started at ~20,000 (2011), grew each year

Why It Ended

In May 2018, organizers announced SCMF would not be held that year. The official statement was vague:

"...the Disco and SMG teams work diligently to ensure every single time you enter the gates of Sun City Music Festival, the best possible experience awaits you. Throughout the calendar year, the SCMF team works extremely hard to meet this momentous task with rigorous standards in place. Unfortunately, we don't believe that goal can be accomplished in 2018." ‚Äî SCMF Official Statement, May 2018

There were hopes of returning in 2019, and Disco Donnie mentioned moving deposits to 2021 during the pandemic, but the festival never resumed. The exact reasons remain unclear ‚Äî possible factors include:

  • Financial challenges in the competitive festival market
  • El Paso's relative isolation making logistics complex
  • Competition from other Labor Day weekend events
  • COVID-19 pandemic disrupting any 2020-2021 return plans

The SCMF Experience

For those who attended, Sun City Music Festival offered something special:

The Setting: Ascarate Park provided an open-air desert venue with mountain views. September in El Paso meant warm days and cool nights ‚Äî ideal festival weather. The park's grass fields and lake created a scenic backdrop unusual for EDM festivals.

The Community: SCMF served a region often overlooked by touring festivals. El Paso locals, along with fans from New Mexico, Arizona, West Texas, and across the Mexican border, created a tight-knit community. The festival was genuinely beloved by its attendees.

The Production: Despite being in a smaller market, SCMF invested in legitimate production. Three stages covered the full spectrum of electronic music ‚Äî main stage for headliners, Beatport stage for underground sounds, and a third for variety. Free water, good sound systems, and solid organization earned praise.

The Value: Affordable compared to major festivals. Travel to El Paso was cheap, hotels were reasonable, and tickets never hit the premium prices of EDC or Ultra. For the lineup quality, it was exceptional value.

"Great two-day electronic music festival with high quality acts. Plenty of room to dance. Free water. Production is amazing each of the two years I've been." ‚Äî Yelp Review, 2017

The Border Connection: El Paso's proximity to Ciudad Ju√°rez meant a significant cross-border audience. SCMF was a cultural bridge, bringing together fans from both sides of the US-Mexico border in celebration of electronic music.
