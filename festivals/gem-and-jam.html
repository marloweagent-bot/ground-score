Before You Go

Gem & Jam requires winter desert preparation:

  • Layer up: Days are warm (60s-70s¬∞F) but nights drop to 30s-40s¬∞F. Bring serious warmth for late-night sets.
  • Research gem shows: The Tucson Gem, Mineral & Fossil Showcase has hundreds of venues across the city.
  • Camping gear: Cold nights mean you need a quality sleeping bag (rated to 20¬∞F or below).
  • Cash for gems: Many gem vendors prefer cash. Come prepared if you want to shop.

Getting There

Tucson is accessible and affordable:

  • Fly in: Tucson International Airport (TUS) is close and often cheaper than Phoenix.
  • Drive: 2 hours from Phoenix, 6 hours from LA. Carpools are common.
  • The venue: Pima County Fairgrounds is easy to find, about 15 minutes from downtown.
  • Camping vs hotels: Camp on-site for full experience, or stay in Tucson and commute daily.

The Four Stages

  • Emerald Stage: Main outdoor stage covered in crystals. Jam bands and headliners. The signature visual.
  • Onyx Stage: Indoor venue with quality production. DJs and electronic acts. Warm escape from cold nights.
  • Quartz Stage: Mix of everything ‚Äî workshops by day, music and performances by night.
  • Opal Stage: Local DJs, silent disco at night (bring headphones or use the app).

The Gem Shows

The Tucson Gem, Mineral & Fossil Showcase is the world's largest ‚Äî and it happens during Gem & Jam:

  • Duration: 3 weeks of gem shows across Tucson (festival is one weekend)
  • Scale: Hundreds of vendors from around the world at dozens of venues
  • On-site: Gem vendors at the festival itself
  • Explore: Take a day to leave the festival and browse the city's mineral markets

Food, Water & Survival

  • Cold is real: Desert nights in February are legitimately cold. Plan accordingly.
  • Food on-site: Standard festival fare at fair prices.
  • Tucson food: Incredible Mexican food and diverse options if you venture into the city.
  • Water: Dry desert air dehydrates. Drink more than you think.

The Crowd

Eclectic and genuine ‚Äî the festival attracts a specific mix:

  • Bass heads + jam fans: Peaceful coexistence of different music tribes
  • Crystal people: The gem show overlap brings mineral enthusiasts
  • Grassroots vets: People who prefer smaller festivals over mega-events
  • Friendly energy: Space is respected, vibes are positive

After the Festival

  • Explore gem shows: The shows run before and after the festival. Budget extra days.
  • Saguaro National Park: Stunning desert scenery just outside Tucson.
  • Warm up: Find good Mexican food and recover in sunshine.
