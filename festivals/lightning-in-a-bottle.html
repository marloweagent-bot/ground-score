Before You Go

LiB requires more preparation than most festivals. Five days of camping demands planning.

  • Camping gear: Bring everything. Tent, sleeping bag, shade structure, cooler. The sun is real in May. Good shade setup is essential.
  • Explore the schedule: LiB isn't just music. Plan which workshops, talks, and activities interest you. The Beacon and Compass stages have transformational programming.
  • Wednesday arrival: Consider the early arrival pass. Thursday is crowded for setup; Wednesday lets you settle in and explore early.
  • Costumes and expression: LiB is creative. Pack outfits that feel like you. Glitter, color, texture ‚Äî express yourself.

Getting There & Getting In

Buena Vista Lake is Central California ‚Äî accessible but requires a drive.

  • From LA: About 2 hours north on I-5. Easy drive, but leave early on opening day.
  • From SF: About 4 hours south. Consider carpooling ‚Äî LiB encourages it.
  • Entry: Thursday morning is the worst time. Aim for Wednesday (early arrival) or Thursday afternoon/evening.
  • Exit: Monday can be slow. Pack up early if you need to leave by a specific time.

The Festival Grounds

The venue wraps around Buena Vista Lake, creating distinct zones to explore.

  • Lightning Stage: The main stage. Biggest production, headliners, and crowds. Good for must-see sets.
  • Thunder Stage: Bass, experimental, and multi-genre. Open-air theater feel with diverse programming.
  • Woogie Stage: House and techno home. Runs Thursday through Sunday with deep lineups. The heartbeat for groove lovers.
  • Compass & Beacon: Workshop areas. Yoga, meditation, talks, interactive sessions. Not music but equally important.
  • The Lake: Yes, you can swim. Bring a towel. Perfect for afternoon cooling off.

Food, Water & Survival

Five days of camping means thinking about sustenance seriously.

  • The Marketplace: LiB food vendors are curated and quality. Expect $12-20 meals, but worth it. Great vegan/vegetarian options.
  • Bring your own: Coolers are allowed. Stock up on breakfast food, snacks, and hydration supplies. Save food money for special meals.
  • Water: Essential. Stations available, but bring a reusable bottle and/or CamelBak. May heat is real.
  • Coffee: Multiple coffee vendors. Morning coffee lines are social scenes themselves.

The Crowd & Community

LiB attracts a specific type of person ‚Äî creative, curious, and open.

  • Age range: Genuinely diverse ‚Äî college kids to 50-somethings. Families with children during the day.
  • Vibe: Less party-hard, more explore-and-connect. People make eye contact and start conversations.
  • Costumes: Elaborate, creative, expressive. Not Halloween costumes ‚Äî more art pieces.
  • Drugs: It's California. Psychedelics are common. Take care of yourself and your crew.

After the Festival

Coming back from LiB is its own experience.

  • Leave No Trace: LiB is serious about sustainability. Clean up your campsite completely. Pack out everything you packed in.
  • Integration: Five days of transformation can hit hard when you return to normal life. Give yourself time to process.
  • The community: LiB friendships often continue. Exchange info with camp neighbors ‚Äî these connections last.
  • Monday recovery: Don't schedule anything important for Monday or Tuesday. You'll need the rest.
