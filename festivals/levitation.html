Origins

The Reverberation Appreciation Society ‚Äî a creative collective including The Black Angels' Christian Bland and Alex Maas ‚Äî launched Austin Psych Fest in March 2008 at The Red Barn in north Austin. Inspired by festivals like ATP and Primavera Sound, they wanted to create a curated celebration of psychedelic music.

The name changed to Levitation in 2015, honoring The 13th Floor Elevators' song and Austin's psychedelic rock history. That same year, the festival hosted the Elevators' first reunion performance in 45 years.

The Electronic Crossover

Levitation isn't an electronic festival, but the psych-electronic boundary is porous:

  • Flying Lotus: Headlined 2016 ‚Äî cosmic beats meet psych visuals
  • Caribou: Electronic-psych crossover perfection
  • The Gaslamp Killer: Beatmaker with psych sensibilities
  • Black Moth Super Rainbow: Analog synths, psych aesthetic
  • Silver Apples: 1960s electronic pioneers

If you like electronic music that thinks, experiments, and refuses easy categorization, Levitation's occasional electronic bookings deliver.

The 2016 Weather Disaster

Severe weather cancelled the 2016 festival at Carson Creek Ranch after significant damage to infrastructure. In response, organizers pivoted to 20+ impromptu shows across Austin's club circuit ‚Äî turning disaster into discovery of a new format.

"The 2016 cancellation accidentally revealed that the multi-venue format worked beautifully. Austin's clubs became the festival." ‚Äî Festival Evolution

Current Format (2024+)

The organizers now run both events annually:

  • Austin Psych Fest (Spring): Single outdoor venue (Far Out Lounge), 3 days, camping available, traditional festival format
  • Levitation (Fall): Multi-venue across Austin clubs, 4 days, city exploration built in

Both celebrate psych music with different experiences. APF is immersive; Levitation is exploratory.

For Electronic Fans: Honest Assessment

If you're looking for EDM, bass music, or mainstream electronic, look elsewhere. Levitation is for:

  • Fans of experimental/ambient electronic (FlyLo, Caribou, Boards of Canada adjacent)
  • People who appreciate psych rock's influence on electronic music
  • Listeners who find genre boundaries boring

Check each year's lineup ‚Äî some years have strong electronic crossover; others lean purely rock. The festival is authentic to its vision, which means electronic isn't guaranteed.

Why It Matters

In a festival landscape dominated by corporate mega-events, Levitation remains independent, curated, and true to its vision. The Reverberation Appreciation Society books what they love, not what algorithms predict. That authenticity creates an experience that feels increasingly rare.

If weird, wonderful, boundary-pushing music appeals to you ‚Äî regardless of genre ‚Äî Levitation deserves attention.
