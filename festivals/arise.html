What Happened

ARISE faced a cascade of challenges that ultimately proved fatal:

  • 2019: Final successful edition at Sunrise Ranch near Loveland
  • 2020: Larimer County denied permit; producers separated
  • 2020-2021: Pandemic hiatus
  • 2022: Festival owner Luke Comer purchased The Cradle property in Boone for a permanent home
  • May 2022: Pueblo County denied special event permit two weeks before the festival
  • June 2022: Permanent cancellation announced

The dream of a permanent festival home ‚Äî owning the land to avoid permit battles ‚Äî ended when the new county also rejected the event.

The Vision

ARISE was built on intentional community values:

"The music is like the fire... and we all gather around it to warm our souls. The intention of the festival is to facilitate and amplify that inspiration into real life action." ‚Äî Paul Bassis, ARISE Producer
  • Conscious performers: Artists selected for alignment with values, not just popularity
  • Diverse activities: Programming beyond music was central, not supplementary
  • Community building: Connection and transformation as explicit goals
  • All ages welcome: Families and multi-generational gathering

The Music

Seven stages hosted an eclectic range of conscious artists:

  • Funk: Lettuce, The Motet, Turkuaz
  • Reggae: Rebelution, SOJA, Trevor Hall
  • Bluegrass/Folk: Rising Appalachia, Elephant Revival
  • Jam: String Cheese Incident, Umphrey's McGee
  • Hip-Hop: Nahko and Medicine for the People
  • Electronic: The Polish Ambassador, Random Rab

The Venues

Sunrise Ranch (2013-2019): Near Loveland, about an hour from Denver. The spiritual community's land provided sweeping vistas and mountain beauty. Permit was eventually denied.

The Cradle (2022): Property purchased in Boone, southern Colorado. The vision of a permanent festival home ended when Pueblo County denied the permit, reportedly over "unforeseen circumstances."

Beyond the Music

ARISE's programming extended far beyond stages:

  • Yoga: Daily practice in mountain settings
  • Speakers: Thought leaders on consciousness, activism, environment
  • Healing: Practitioners offering bodywork and energy sessions
  • Workshops: Skills and practices for conscious living
  • Art: Installations, live painting, interactive experiences

The Legacy

ARISE proved that conscious values and quality festival production could coexist. For seven years, it gathered Colorado's conscious community and created lasting bonds. The 2013 edition was called "Colorado's best festival of the year" by attendees who recognized something special.

The community that gathered at ARISE didn't disappear ‚Äî it dispersed to other festivals, maintained connections, and carries forward the intention of community through creativity. The festival may be gone, but its spirit lives in those who experienced it.

For Those Who Remember

If you attended ARISE, you know what was lost. If you never made it, understand that this was a gathering where the values were real, the community was genuine, and the music served something larger than entertainment.

Colorado's conscious music scene continues at other festivals, but ARISE left a specific hole that hasn't been filled. May something arise in its place.
