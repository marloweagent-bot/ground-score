Before You Go

Okeechobee is a commitment ‚Äî four days in rural Florida with no easy escape. That's the point.

  • Tickets: GA includes camping. VIP adds viewing areas and upgraded facilities. Payment plans available.
  • Camping gear: You need a tent, sleeping setup, chairs, shade structure. The grove has natural shade but bring a canopy for your campsite.
  • Car camping vs GA: Car camping lets you keep your vehicle at your site. Worth the upgrade.
  • March weather: Days in the 80s, nights in the 50s-60s. Bring layers. Rain is possible.

Getting There & Getting In

Sunshine Grove is between Miami and Orlando ‚Äî not close to either. Plan accordingly.

  • Fly into: Palm Beach International (PBI) is closest at 1.5 hours. Miami (MIA) and Orlando (MCO) are both 2+ hours.
  • Driving: Essential. Limited rideshare availability in rural Florida.
  • Arrival: Thursday is move-in day. Arrive early to claim a good campsite. Lines can be long but move steadily.
  • What to bring: Check the website's camping essentials list. No glass, grills have rules, coolers allowed.

The Festival Grounds

The grove layout creates distinct zones with natural flow between them.

  • Be Here Now: Main stage with major headliners. Open field with good sightlines.
  • Here Stage: Second stage for rock, indie, and mid-tier acts.
  • Now Stage: Electronic and bass music focus. Gets heavy at night.
  • Jungle 51: The legendary hidden stage deep in the woods. House and techno until sunrise. Finding it is part of the experience.
  • Chobeewobee Village: Daytime activities, yoga, workshops, food, art.

Food, Water & Survival

You're camping in Florida. Hydration is survival.

  • Bring a cooler: Stock up before you arrive. Campsite meals save money and energy.
  • Water: Free refill stations throughout. Bring a hydration pack or large water bottle.
  • Food vendors: Good variety inside the festival. Cuban food, BBQ, healthy options. Plan to spend $15-20 per meal.
  • General store: On-site for forgotten essentials at festival prices.

The Crowd & Community

Okeechobee attracts a specific type: open-minded music lovers who value experience over image.

  • Age range: Mostly 21-35, but all ages welcome (18+ to enter).
  • Vibe: More Burning Man than Ultra. Costumes encouraged, judgment discouraged.
  • Camp neighbors: Half the magic is who you camp near. Be friendly.
  • Multi-genre: You'll meet jam band kids and bass heads and hip-hop fans ‚Äî often in the same person.

After the Festival

Monday is pack-out day. Leave no trace.

  • Recovery: Don't book an early flight Monday. You'll be exhausted and need time to break camp.
  • Nearby: Lake Okeechobee is right there if you want to extend your Florida adventure.
  • The hangover: Post-Okee depression is real. The community stays connected online between years.
