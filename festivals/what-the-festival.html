Origins

What the Festival was founded by Glen and Tiffany Boyd, who sold their web analytics company WebTrends in the early 2000s and brought their Burning Man camp experience to Oregon.

  • 2012: First WTF held at White River Canyon near Tygh Valley
  • 2013: Moved to Wolf Run Ranch (250 acres purchased by organizers) near Dufur
  • 2013-2019: Grew into the Pacific Northwest's premiere boutique festival
  • 2019: Final edition ‚Äî organizers announced the festival would not return

What Made It Special

WTF was designed from the ground up as an immersive art experience that happened to have great music.

"WTF wasn't just a festival ‚Äî it was a portal. You stepped through the gates and reality shifted. Every corner was designed to surprise, delight, or make you question what you just saw." ‚Äî Festival Squad, 2016
  • Funktion-One sound: World-class audio throughout, not just main stage
  • Immersive art: Burning Man-caliber installations in an accessible setting
  • Boutique scale: ~5,000 people meant you saw familiar faces by day 2
  • Oregon twist: Pacific Northwest aesthetic and values
  • Summer solstice: Longest days of the year added magic

The Ending

In 2019, organizers announced that WTF would not return. The exact reasons were never fully detailed, but the festival community speculated about rising costs, burnout, and the increasingly difficult festival landscape.

  • The website now reads: "What The Festival Will Not Be Returning. Thank you for all the amazing memories."
  • Wolf Run Ranch: The property remains, but no successor festival has emerged
  • Community impact: Left a genuine void in the PNW festival scene

Spiritual Successors

For those seeking WTF's energy, some festivals carry similar DNA:

  • Lightning in a Bottle: California's transformational festival with similar art focus
  • Symbiosis / The Bloom: When active, carried similar West Coast transformational vibes
  • Shambhala: Canadian festival with strong art/community emphasis
  • Regional Burns: Oregon regional burns carry some of the same energy
  • Beloved Festival: Oregon gathering with community focus (smaller scale)
