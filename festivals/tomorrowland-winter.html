Before You Go

This is a week-long commitment combining two demanding activities. Prepare accordingly.

  • Book packages early: Accommodation is bundled. Budget, mid-range, and luxury options. Sells out fast.
  • Ski ability: All levels welcome. Beginners can take lessons. But if you can't ski, you're missing half the experience.
  • Fitness: Skiing all day + dancing all night = exhaustion. Pace yourself or pick your priorities.
  • Gear: Proper ski clothing essential. Evenings are cold. Layering is key.

Getting There & Around

Alpe d'Huez is a proper ski resort in the French Alps, not the easiest to reach.

  • Airports: Lyon (LYS) or Geneva (GVA), 2-3 hours by transfer.
  • Packages include transfers: Book through Tomorrowland for seamless logistics.
  • On-site: Gondolas and ski lifts connect the resort. Some walking between venues.
  • Altitude: Resort sits at 1,860m. Acclimatize, stay hydrated.

The Festival Experience

Days flow between mountain adventures and musical moments.

  • Daytime: Ski the 250km of runs. Surprise DJ sets at mountain-top bars. Apr√®s-ski parties start afternoon.
  • Mainstage: Iconic Tomorrowland production adapted for altitude. Evening shows against sunset over peaks.
  • Indoor stages: Clubs and venues throughout the resort for late-night sessions.
  • La Folie Douce: The legendary apr√®s-ski venue goes all day with DJ sets.

Food, Drink & Survival

Full ski resort infrastructure means quality options but premium prices.

  • Mountain restaurants: Proper sit-down meals on the slopes. Raclette, fondue, tartiflette.
  • Village dining: From quick bites to gourmet. Package meals available.
  • Hydration: Altitude + alcohol + dancing = dehydration risk. Water, water, water.
  • Recovery: Many accommodations have spa access. You'll need it.

The Crowd & Community

Tomorrowland's international community, filtered through a shared love of winter sports.

  • Demographics: Affluent, international, 25-40 skewing older than summer TML.
  • Vibe: Ski culture's casual friendliness meets festival euphoria. Easy to meet people on lifts.
  • Dress code: Ski gear by day, festival fashion (with layers) by night.

Pro Tips

  • Don't try to do everything: Pick ski days vs party days. You can't peak for 7 days straight.
  • Explore the resort: Some best moments happen at random pop-up sets on the mountain.
  • Book dining: Popular restaurants fill up. Reserve ahead.
  • Bring good headphones: Silent disco gondola rides are a thing.
