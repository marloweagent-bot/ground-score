Before You Go

Bonnaroo is a marathon in Tennessee heat. Preparation is everything.

  • Tent setup: Get a reflective tarp to go OVER your tent. Direct sun = sauna by 8 AM. You won't sleep past sunrise without shade.
  • Camping gear: EZ-up canopy is essential. Brings shade to your campsite. Don't skip this.
  • Hydration plan: Bring a CamelBak AND extra water jugs. Electrolyte packets. You will sweat more than you've ever sweat.
  • Schedule The Other: Electronic programming is heavily loaded after midnight. Plan to sleep during afternoon heat and rally for late-night.

Getting There & Getting In

Rural Tennessee means driving. Plan for traffic and early arrival.

  • By car: Most people drive. Interstate access is straightforward. Arrival lines can be hours long on Thursday.
  • By plane: Nashville (BNA) is closest. Rent a car or book official shuttle (shuttles run limited schedules).
  • Arrival timing: Wednesday arrival exists for early access (extra cost). Thursday morning = long wait. Arrive before dawn or accept the line.
  • Camping zones: You're assigned a pod area. Premium camping (closer to Centeroo) costs more but saves walking.

The Festival Grounds

Know your electronic zones. They're spread across 700 acres.

  • The Other: Your home base. Large open-air stage with full production. Runs late-night into sunrise. All major electronic acts play here.
  • Infinity Stage: New in 2025. Additional dance-focused stage with its own programming.
  • Where in the Woods: Campground stage that keeps going after Centeroo closes. More intimate, underground vibes.
  • What Stage: Main stage. Occasionally books electronic headliners (Pretty Lights 2024). Capacity 80K.
  • The distance problem: The Other is a solid walk from main camping. Budget 20-30 minutes each way.

Food, Water & Survival

Tennessee in June will humble you. Plan accordingly.

  • Water strategy: Fill up at every station. Bring a CamelBak AND a water jug at camp. Dehydration sneaks up on you.
  • Cooling off: The Fountain in Centeroo ‚Äî you will end up in it. Pack a swimsuit or clothes you don't mind getting wet.
  • Food timing: Eat before going to The Other for late-night sets. Centeroo food closes eventually.
  • Amish donuts: Legendary. Worth the line. Budget for multiple visits.
  • Coffee: If you're doing sunrise sets, you'll need it. Multiple vendors throughout.

The Crowd & Community

Bonnaroo's community is its greatest strength. Lean into it.

  • Camp neighbors: You'll be next to the same people for 4 days. Introduce yourselves. Share supplies. This is how Roo works.
  • The Other crowd: Late-night brings out the committed. 3 AM sets = real fans, good energy.
  • Mixed genres: You'll meet jam band people, indie kids, electronic heads. Cross-pollinate. Discover new music.
  • Radiate Positivity: It's not just a slogan. The culture is genuinely warm. People help each other.

After the Festival

Four days of farm life takes its toll. Plan recovery.

  • Don't drive home Sunday: You'll be exhausted. Book a hotel in Manchester or Nashville for Sunday night.
  • Shower: Your first real shower will be life-changing. Don't underestimate this.
  • Leave early Monday: Beat the exodus. Everyone leaves at the same time otherwise.
  • Post-Roo depression: It's real. The community, the music, the escape ‚Äî real life hits hard. Stay connected with the people you met.
