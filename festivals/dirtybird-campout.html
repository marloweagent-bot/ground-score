Before You Go

Dirtybird Campout is a camping festival ‚Äî embrace the summer camp energy.

  • Pack for activities: Bring costumes, crafts, game gear. Participation makes it better.
  • Camping essentials: Tent, sleeping bag, shade, cooler. Car camping is the move.
  • River ready: Pack swimwear and water shoes. The Eel River is a highlight.
  • Prepare for community: This is social. Leave the introvert shell at home.

Getting There & Getting In

Cook's Valley is in Humboldt County ‚Äî stunningly beautiful but legitimately remote.

  • From San Francisco: 4+ hours north. Scenic drive through wine country and redwoods.
  • From Sacramento: About the same. No shortcuts ‚Äî enjoy the journey.
  • Carpools encouraged: The drive is long but the company makes it better.
  • Gas up early: Stations become sparse in Humboldt. Don't push your luck.

The Festival Grounds

A real campground transformed into a music festival playground.

  • Main Stage: The big acts, sunset sets, crowd energy.
  • The Bunker: Late-night underground vibes. The real heads know.
  • Activity Areas: Spread throughout ‚Äî spelling bee stage, sports fields, craft zones.
  • The River: Swimming, floating, cooling off. Essential during hot days.

Food, Water & Survival

Camping festival logistics apply ‚Äî come prepared.

  • Bring a cooler: Stock up before you arrive. Options onsite are limited.
  • Ice runs: Available but plan ahead. Melting happens fast.
  • Water stations: Free and plentiful. Bring a reusable bottle.
  • River hydration: Swimming keeps you cool. Dance, swim, repeat.

The Crowd & Community

The "flock" is a real community that returns year after year.

  • Playful energy: Adults acting like kids (in the best way). Embrace the silly.
  • Inclusive vibe: Weird is welcome. Judgment is not.
  • Participatory culture: Join activities, meet people, make memories.
  • Discord community: The flock stays connected year-round online.
