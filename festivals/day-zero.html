What Is Day Zero?

Day Zero is Damian Lazarus's vision of electronic music as ceremony. Founded in 2012, it's timed to the Mayan calendar's symbolic "rebirth" ‚Äî a celebration of new cycles and transformation.

  • Philosophy: Music, nature, spirituality, community intertwined
  • Curator: Damian Lazarus (Crosstown Rebels founder, underground legend)
  • Format: Single night, dusk until well past dawn
  • Climax: Damian's legendary sunrise set as light breaks through jungle canopy

For US Festival-Goers

Day Zero is not in the US, but many Americans make the pilgrimage:

  • Flights: Cancun (CUN) is the main hub. Direct flights from major US cities ($400-800 roundtrip).
  • Ground transport: 90-minute drive from Cancun to Tulum. Shuttles, private transfers, or rental cars.
  • Stay duration: Most people make it a week-long trip. Tulum has world-class beaches, restaurants, cenotes, Mayan ruins.
  • January timing: Perfect winter escape from US cold. Weather is ideal (75-85¬∞F).
  • Passport: Required for Mexico entry.

The Night Experience

The jungle awakens at dusk:

  • Arrival: Shuttles from Tulum town. Enter as sun sets.
  • Three stages: Main stage, secondary stage, El Teatro (Mexican artists)
  • Journey: Music builds through the night, energy shifting hour by hour
  • Climax (6-9am): Damian Lazarus takes the stage as dawn approaches
  • Sunrise: The whole crowd watches light filter through jungle canopy together
  • Departure: Late morning, transformed

Related Events

The Day Zero universe extends beyond the main event:

  • Get Lost (Miami/NYC): Damian's warehouse party concept ‚Äî US-accessible underground experience
  • Crosstown Rebels nights: Label events in major cities worldwide
  • Tulum season: January is peak season with multiple events (Zamna, etc.)
  • Day Zero international: Occasional editions in other locations globally

Planning Your Pilgrimage

  • Book early: Tickets sell out. Tulum hotels book up for January season.
  • Budget for the trip: Flights + hotel + festival + food = $2,000-4,000+ for a week
  • Extend your stay: Don't fly out the next day. You'll need recovery time, and Tulum deserves exploration.
  • What to bring: Comfortable shoes for jungle terrain, layers for cool night/warm dawn, open mind
  • What to expect: Not EDC. Not Ultra. Something slower, deeper, more sacred.

Is It Worth the Trip?

If you resonate with:

  • Underground house and techno (not mainstream EDM)
  • Nature-integrated experiences
  • Spiritual/conscious community
  • Sunrise sets and dusk-to-dawn journeys
  • Destination festival vacations

Then Day Zero may be the most meaningful festival experience you'll ever have. For mainstream EDM fans, look elsewhere. For seekers, this is a pilgrimage worth making at least once.
