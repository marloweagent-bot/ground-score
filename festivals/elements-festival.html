Before You Go

Elements rewards preparation more than most festivals.

  • Boots, not shoes: Mud is a real possibility. Bring rain boots and expect the worst.
  • Arrive early Thursday: Parking and camping logistics are smoother before the Friday rush.
  • VIP worth considering: Multiple attendees say VIP significantly improves the experience.
  • Weather prep: August in PA means heat, humidity, and thunderstorms. All three.

Getting There & Getting In

Accessibility is the festival's superpower. Execution... less so.

  • From NYC: ~2 hours. Easy day-of drive or take the shuttle.
  • From Philly: ~2 hours. Similarly accessible.
  • Parking: Available but can be chaotic. Arrive early, bring patience.
  • Entry: Security varies. Lines can be long. Plan accordingly.

The Festival Grounds

Seven stages themed around the four elements (yes, the math doesn't quite work).

  • Fire Stage: Main stage, headliners, biggest productions.
  • Water/Funktapus: Immersive underwater-themed bass stage. Dubstep, D&B, heavy stuff.
  • Earth Stage: Has had sound quality complaints ‚Äî enter with managed expectations.
  • Air + Others: House, tech house, more intimate vibes.
  • Sound Camps: Where the real late-night magic happens. Don't sleep on these.
"The sound camps not only salvaged Friday night but kept the party going all weekend long. Notable sets included Boogie T, TVBOO b2b INZO, and three Tape B sets. Sometimes the renegades are better than main stages." ‚Äî Festival review, 2024

Food, Water & Survival

Plan for self-sufficiency.

  • Water: Bring a CamelBak. Stations exist but lines happen.
  • Food: Standard festival vendors. Bring camp food to save money and time.
  • Portapotties: Have been notably bad. Pack baby wipes and hand sanitizer.
  • Heat: August = sweating. Shade structures for camp are essential.

The Crowd & Community

Mixed bag reflecting the NYC metro area.

  • Diverse: All types from scene kids to veterans to first-timers.
  • Bass stages: Generally better vibes than main stage crowds.
  • Sound camps: Where the community shines ‚Äî smaller, friendlier, realer.
  • Scene element: NYC proximity means some "see and be seen" energy.

After the Festival

Recovery time matters.

  • Monday traffic: Leave early or very late. Mid-morning is brutal.
  • Shower immediately: Three days of August humidity demands it.
  • Process the experience: Elements is polarizing ‚Äî your crew will have takes.
  • Feedback surveys: They actually read them. Help improve next year.
