The Birth

Stephen Rehage launched Voodoo on October 30, 1999 at Tad Gormley Stadium. The first lineup featured Wyclef Jean and Moby ‚Äî prescient booking that signaled the festival's multi-genre DNA from day one.

The Halloween timing was intentional. New Orleans already had Mardi Gras and Jazz Fest; Voodoo gave the city a fall anchor event that played into its gothic, mysterious reputation.

The Electronic Legacy

Unlike many rock festivals that added EDM reluctantly, Voodoo embraced electronic music early:

  • Ti√´sto: Multiple appearances, massive production
  • Deadmau5: Perfect fit for Halloween theming
  • Calvin Harris: 2013's 15th anniversary headliner
  • Bassnectar: Multiple years of heavy bass
  • Afrojack, Boys Noize, Big Gigantic: Deep electronic booking

The "Le Ritual" area became the home for heavier rock while electronic acts often played the larger stages alongside rock headliners.

Post-Katrina Resilience

When Hurricane Katrina devastated New Orleans in 2005, Voodoo relocated part of the festival to Memphis while still holding a tribute event in Audubon Park for relief workers. Nine Inch Nails and Queens of the Stone Age performed at the first major multi-artist event in the city post-Katrina.

The C3/Live Nation Era

Live Nation acquired a majority stake in 2013; C3 Presents (Austin City Limits, Lollapalooza) took over production. The festival professionalized ‚Äî bigger stages, more efficient operations, higher-profile bookings. Some felt it lost indie character; others appreciated the polish.

Peak attendance hit 180,000+ in 2018, making it one of the largest festivals in the country.

The Mysterious End

2019 was the last Voodoo. COVID cancelled 2020 and 2021. Then, in 2022, the festival was simply cancelled without explanation. No statement, no "pause," just silence.

Rumors suggest:

  • Legal disputes between founder Stephen Rehage and Live Nation
  • Economics not working post-COVID
  • Strategic shifts in Live Nation's festival portfolio

As of early 2024, some sources suggest a possible 2025 return, but nothing is confirmed. The Voodoo subreddit watches and waits.

What Made It Irreplaceable

No other festival combined these elements:

  • Halloween timing: Built-in costume culture, spooky atmosphere
  • New Orleans setting: The city is the experience; French Quarter afterparties are unmatched
  • True multi-genre: Rock, electronic, hip-hop, and local NOLA acts all valued equally
  • 21-year history: Institutional memory, returning fans, NOLA tradition

If Voodoo returns, it'll be welcomed with open arms. Until then, Halloween in New Orleans continues without its anchor festival.
