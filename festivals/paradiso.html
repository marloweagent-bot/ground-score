What Made It Special

Paradiso wasn't just a festival ‚Äî it was a pilgrimage. People planned their summers around it. The combination of factors was unmatched:

  • The Gorge: Rolling in and seeing the canyon for the first time was transformative
  • Camping community: Arriving Thursday, leaving Monday ‚Äî you were fully immersed
  • Sunset sets: Watching artists play as the sun dropped behind the gorge
  • PNW culture: The Pacific Northwest scene is passionate and welcoming
"All-in-all Paradiso felt like a dream. My escape from familiar territory thrust me into the most beautiful setting I've ever been. I made so many new friends, saw some of my favorite DJs, and got to do it all at the Gorge." ‚Äî Festival review, 2017

The Fall: What Happened

Paradiso's end was ugly and complicated:

  • 2019 sold out as usual, but behind the scenes, things were falling apart
  • USC Events (promoter) failed to pay artists and vendors properly
  • Insomniac (co-promoter) sued USC in January 2020 for $2 million in unpaid obligations
  • Live Nation (Gorge owner) confirmed Paradiso would not return to the venue
  • USC Events went silent and ceased operations

Many vendors, employees, and artists were left unpaid. The community that built Paradiso was betrayed by mismanagement.

The Camping Experience

Camping was mandatory (no day passes) and fundamental to the experience:

  • Standard Camping: Basic grass lot, bring your gear, community forms naturally
  • Premier Camping: Better location, more space, some amenities
  • Gold Camping: Premium spots with additional perks
  • Terrace Camping: The best views and exclusive access

The campgrounds had their own stages, vendors, and programming. The party started Thursday and didn't stop until Monday morning.

The Future: What Comes Next?

Insomniac's CEO has hinted at bringing an event to The Gorge to replace Paradiso. Beyond Wonderland PNW? EDC expansion? Nothing confirmed yet, but the demand is there:

  • Venue available: The Gorge hosts other festivals (Sasquatch was there for years)
  • Demand proven: Paradiso sold out consistently ‚Äî the market exists
  • Insomniac interest: Public statements suggest they want to fill this gap
  • Community ready: PNW ravers are waiting for something to replace what they lost
