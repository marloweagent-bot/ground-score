Before You Go

JTMF is not a "show up and rage" festival. It rewards preparation and the right mindset.

  • Set expectations: This is not EDM. It's world music, global sounds, and eclectic acts. Come to discover, not to hear your Spotify playlist.
  • Pack for extremes: Desert days are hot (especially May), nights get cold. Layers are essential. Dust happens.
  • Book early: The festival sells out and capacity is limited. Loyalty tickets go fast.
  • Embrace the pace: This festival moves slower. That's intentional. Leave the FOMO at home.

Getting There & Getting In

The venue (Joshua Tree Lake) is about 10 minutes from Joshua Tree village and an hour from Palm Springs.

  • Fly into Palm Springs (PSP): Closest airport, ~50 minutes to venue. Rent a car.
  • LA airports: LAX/Burbank are 2-2.5 hours. Doable, but plan for traffic.
  • Car camping: Drive up to your spot. Easy in, easy out. No shuttle nightmares.
  • Entry: Relaxed vibe. No TSA-style gauntlet. Check-in is smooth.

The Festival Grounds

Compact and walkable. Everything is designed for human-scale interaction, not crowd control.

  • Main Stage: Where headliners play. Still intimate by festival standards ‚Äî you can actually see faces.
  • Secondary stages: Smaller, more experimental acts. Often where the real discoveries happen.
  • Workshop areas: Yoga, movement, talks, healing arts scattered throughout.
  • Community spaces: Art installations, shaded hangouts, places designed for conversation.

Food, Water & Survival

The desert is unforgiving. Respect it.

  • Hydrate aggressively: You're sweating more than you realize. Start before you arrive.
  • Water stations: Available throughout. Bring a reusable bottle.
  • Food vendors: Eclectic mix matching the musical vibe. Vegetarian/vegan-friendly. Reasonable prices.
  • Bring snacks: Car camping means you can have your own cooler. Use it.

The Crowd & Community

This is what makes JTMF special. The community self-selects for good humans.

  • Age range: Genuinely all ages ‚Äî 20-somethings dancing next to families with kids.
  • Vibe: Relaxed, curious, open. People make eye contact and say hello.
  • Substances: Much lighter than typical festival scene. People come for the experience, not to get wrecked.
  • Return rate: Extremely high. Veterans call it "coming home."

After the Festival

You're in one of America's most beautiful regions. Extend the trip.

  • Joshua Tree National Park: 10 minutes away. Hike, climb, stare at rocks. Essential.
  • Palm Springs: Pool, spa, mid-century modern architecture for recovery.
  • Integration: The festival is designed to be contemplative. Give yourself time to process.
