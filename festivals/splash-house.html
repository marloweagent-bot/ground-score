The Three Hotels

Your pass gets you into all three pools. Each has its own character:

  • Saguaro: The original, the iconic. Rainbow-painted walls, best crowd, typically better DJ bookings. The most "Splash House" of the three. Can get crowded but worth it.
  • Renaissance: The oasis. More resort-feeling, towers with balconies, pristine pools. Good for recovery between sets or when Saguaro is packed.
  • Hilton (NEW 2026): Replaces the Riviera. Central location between Renaissance and Saguaro. Unknown quantity but promising.

Before You Go

  • Hotel booking strategy: Host hotels (Saguaro, Renaissance, Hilton) eliminate shuttle needs but book fast and cost more. Off-site hotels are cheaper but require shuttle logistics.
  • Two weekends: August 7-9 and August 14-16 are separate events with different lineups. Pick one or go both (different passes).
  • After Hours tickets: Separate from pool passes. $100+ but worth it ‚Äî the Air Museum tarmac is the highlight for many.
  • Heat prep: It's August in the desert. 100¬∞F+ is standard. Sunscreen, hydration, and pool access are essential.
  • Alumni access: Returning attendees get early ticket access. Sign up for the email list.

Getting There

  • Palm Springs Airport (PSP): Small, easy, 15 minutes from hotels. Limited flights but growing.
  • LAX/SNA: 2-hour drive through the desert. Scenic but plan for traffic.
  • Shuttles: Free shuttles between the three hotels run all day. Also to/from After Hours.
  • Parking: If driving, hotels have parking. Some charge fees during Splash House weekends.

The Day Experience

Typical Splash House day flow:

  • Gates open ~noon: Pools start filling. Morning is recovery time.
  • Peak hours (2-6pm): Best music, biggest crowds, hottest weather. Claim your pool spot early.
  • Pool hopping: Shuttles between hotels take 10-20 minutes. You can see 2-3 pools in a day if ambitious.
  • Pools close ~6pm: Daytime portion ends. Time to eat, nap, prep for After Hours.
  • After Hours (10pm-2am): Separate venue at Palm Springs Air Museum. Dancing on tarmac under vintage aircraft.

Survival Tips

  • Hydrate aggressively: Desert heat + alcohol + dancing = dehydration. Water, electrolytes, shade breaks.
  • Sunscreen every 2 hours: You're in water, in the sun, all day. Reapply constantly.
  • Swim gear: Bring what you'd bring to a pool party. Floaties, sunglasses, waterproof phone case.
  • Lockers: Available at venues. Essential for storing valuables while you're in the pool.
  • Pace yourself: It's a 3-day event. Don't destroy yourself on Friday.

The Crowd

Know who you're splashing with:

  • Age: 21+ requirement means mature crowd (24-35 core demographic)
  • Vibe: Social, fun, attractive, house music fans
  • SoCal-heavy: Many LA and San Diego locals, plus tourists
  • Regulars: Strong repeat attendee culture ‚Äî 13 years of devotees

After Hours: The Air Museum

The night portion that many consider the highlight:

  • Location: Palm Springs Air Museum ‚Äî a tarmac with vintage military aircraft
  • Hours: Friday & Saturday nights, 10pm-2am
  • Separate tickets: $100+ on top of pool passes
  • Vibe: More "real festival" energy ‚Äî proper stage, cooler temperatures, dancing
  • Highlight: Dancing under the stars with San Jacinto mountains as backdrop
