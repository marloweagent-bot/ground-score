Why It Mattered (Just Not For Electronic Fans)

Pitchfork Music Festival was culturally significant for indie music. It launched careers, championed emerging artists, and created a community around music criticism and discovery. But let's be clear:

  • Electronic programming was minimal: In most years, you'd find 0-3 electronic acts across the entire lineup.
  • Jamie xx 2024 was an exception: His headlining set was notable BECAUSE it was unusual for the festival.
  • Aftershows had more: Artists like Doss played late-night shows at Chicago clubs, but that's separate from the festival.
  • Wrong audience: The crowd came for Alanis Morissette, Black Pumas, Brittany Howard ‚Äî not bass drops.

What Electronic Fans Missed (Nothing, Really)

If you skipped Pitchfork because of the lineup, you made the right call for your interests.

  • No dedicated electronic stage: Unlike festivals that carve out space for dance music, Pitchfork integrated occasional acts into the general lineup.
  • Daytime hours: Festival ended at 10PM. No late-night sets, no sunrise experiences.
  • Chill energy: Crowds were attentive but not raging. Great for appreciating a folk set, less great for losing yourself in beats.
"I went for Jamie xx in 2024 and spent most of the weekend wondering why I was there. The music was good but it wasn't my music. Should've just caught him on tour." ‚Äî r/electronicmusic, 2024

Alternatives For Chicago Electronic Fans

If you want electronic music in Chicago, here's where to actually go:

  • Spring Awakening: Dedicated EDM festival in Chicago. This was your actual move.
  • North Coast: Multi-genre but with strong electronic programming. R.I.P. to the original format.
  • Smartbar: Chicago's legendary house/techno club. Year-round quality bookings.
  • Spybar: Intimate techno venue with international acts.
  • ARC Music Festival: Chicago's newer house/techno focused fest. This is your answer.

Legacy

Pitchfork's closure marks the end of an era for indie music festivals. For electronic fans, it's less of a loss ‚Äî the festival was never really for you. But respect to what it accomplished for its community.

  • 19 years of curation: Consistently booked artists who mattered to the indie world.
  • Affordable and accessible: Compared to mega-festivals, Pitchfork kept prices reasonable.
  • Chicago institution: Part of the city's music identity alongside Lollapalooza and Riot Fest.
