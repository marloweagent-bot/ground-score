Before You Go

Camping festivals require more preparation than single-day events.

  • Camping gear: Tent, sleeping bag, shade structure, cooler. Glen Helen can be hot during the day ‚Äî shade is essential.
  • Car camping vs tent: Car camping lets you sleep in AC if you have a suitable vehicle. Worth considering for comfort.
  • Group coordination: Camping with friends? Coordinate arrival times to camp together. First-come, first-served on spots.
  • Friday arrival: Get there early on day one. Setting up camp in the dark is miserable.

Getting There & Getting In

Glen Helen requires driving ‚Äî plan accordingly.

  • From LA: About 60 miles east. 1 hour without traffic, but Friday afternoon can be rough.
  • Parking: On-site for campers. Day pass holders have separate parking areas.
  • Camping check-in: Can be slow on Friday afternoon. Budget extra time. Once you're set up, you're set for the weekend.
  • Festival entry: Camping wristband lets you enter the venue. Security checks, bag searches ‚Äî standard Insomniac protocols.

The Festival Grounds

Glen Helen's natural amphitheater creates distinct zones and elevation changes.

  • Main Stage: The big production, headline acts, largest crowds. In the natural bowl of the amphitheater.
  • Secondary Stages: Genre-specific programming ‚Äî bass, house, trance represented. More intimate vibes.
  • Art installations: Scattered throughout the venue, glowing after dark. The Wonderland theming is immersive.
  • Campgrounds: Adjacent to the venue. Short walk back to your tent for rest or supplies.

Food, Water & Survival

Camping means self-sufficiency is possible and recommended.

  • Bring your own: Coolers with food and drinks for camp. Save money, eat better, have control over your meals.
  • Inside the venue: Standard festival vendors if you want hot food. Prices are typical ($12-18 entrees).
  • Water: Critical. September can be hot. Bring extra water for camp. Refill stations in the venue.
  • Weather: Hot days, cool nights. Pack layers. Shade structures for camp are essential.

The Crowd & Community

Nocturnal's camping element creates a unique community.

  • Camping culture: Your neighbors become friends. Pre-parties at campsites, shared meals, collective experiences.
  • Age range: Mix of veterans who've been coming for years and younger ravers discovering the festival.
  • PLUR: Strong at Nocturnal. The camping community reinforces positive vibes ‚Äî you'll see these people all weekend.
  • Accessibility: More manageable crowds than EDC. Easier to meet people and have conversations.

After the Festival

Pack-out and recovery are part of the camping experience.

  • Sunday night: Most people leave Monday morning. Sunday night at camp is a wind-down with your crew.
  • Pack-out: Don't leave trash. Clean your campsite. Respect the venue so the festival can return.
  • Recovery: You'll be tired. Don't plan anything important for Monday. Sleep and hydrate.
  • Post-festival depression: Coming back to normal life after a camping festival hits different. Give yourself grace.
