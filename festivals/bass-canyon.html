Before You Go

Bass Canyon requires planning. You're going to the middle of Washington to camp for four nights.

  • Camping is mandatory: There are no hotels within an hour. You're bringing a tent or renting gear on-site.
  • Rent a car: There's no other way to get there. Seattle airport + rental is the move for most people.
  • Bring everything: The nearest store is far. Pack all camping supplies, food, water, sunscreen, layers.
  • Wednesday early arrival: Get the best campsites and settle in before the crowds. Highly recommended.
  • Hearing protection: Excision's system will damage your ears. Quality earplugs are mandatory.

Getting There & Getting In

The Gorge is remote. Plan accordingly.

  • From Seattle: About 2.5 hours east on I-90. Beautiful drive through the Cascades.
  • From Portland: About 4 hours north. Longer but works.
  • Fly + rent: SEA-TAC is your airport. Book a car that fits your camping gear.
  • Camping tiers: Standard, Premier, Terrace‚Äîeach closer to the venue. Premier/Terrace worth it for shorter walks.
  • Check-in: Campground entry can be slow. Arrive early in the day if possible.

The Festival Grounds

The Gorge Amphitheatre is a natural bowl carved into the Columbia River canyon.

  • Main Stage (Paradox): Excision's full touring production with the Gorge as backdrop. This is where the magic happens. All headliners here.
  • Second Stage: Smaller stage in the upper bowl area for additional artists. Great for discovery and smaller crowds.
  • The View: The Columbia River Gorge drops 900 feet behind the stage. Sunset sets are transcendent. You'll understand when you see it.
  • Lawn vs Pit: Lawn seating gives you the full view. Pit gets you close to the action. Both experiences are valid.

Food, Water & Survival

Remote location means self-sufficiency is important.

  • Bring food for camp: Coolers, easy-cook meals, snacks. You don't want to rely entirely on vendors.
  • Water: Bring plenty for camp. Fill stations in the venue. The high desert climate dehydrates you faster than you expect.
  • Weather prep: August days are warm (80s-90s), nights get cold (50s-60s). Bring layers and sleeping bag rated for cold.
  • Dust: The Gorge is dusty. Bring bandanas and prepare for it in your hair, tent, everything.
  • Sunscreen: High altitude sun is intense. Reapply throughout the day.

The Crowd & Community

Bass Canyon draws dedicated bass music fans who create an incredible community.

  • Pacific Northwest bass scene: Seattle, Portland, and Vancouver crews form the core. Welcoming to visitors.
  • Smaller = tighter: 25,000 is intimate compared to Lost Lands' 45,000. You'll see the same people and make friends.
  • Headbanger culture: These people know the music. Respect the scene and you'll be embraced.
  • Camping community: Your neighbors become family. Share food, share stories, share the experience.

After the Festival

Don't rush out. The Gorge area has more to offer.

  • Monday recovery: Most people leave Monday. The drive back to Seattle is beautiful‚Äîdon't rush it hungover.
  • Explore the region: Leavenworth (Bavarian village), Columbia River Gorge hikes, Seattle itself. Make a trip of it.
  • Post-festival depression: Bass Canyon hits hard emotionally. The comedown is real. Stay connected with new friends online.
  • Book early for next year: Tickets sell fast. If you loved it, secure your spot before you leave.
