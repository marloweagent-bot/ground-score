Before You Go

Escape is about the costume as much as the music. Plan accordingly.

  • Costume strategy: Go elaborate but danceable. You'll be moving for 8+ hours. Heavy masks and restrictive outfits become torture by midnight.
  • Backup costume: Bring a simpler option for night 2 if your main costume is complex. Many people do "big" night 1, comfortable night 2.
  • Study the lineup: Four stages means conflicts. Know your must-sees and which stages they're at.
  • Hotel booking: San Bernardino hotels are cheap but basic. Some people stay in Ontario or even LA and drive.

Getting There & Getting In

NOS Events Center is in San Bernardino ‚Äî not exactly central, but accessible.

  • From LA: About 60 miles east. Without traffic: 1 hour. Friday Halloween weekend traffic: 2-3 hours. Leave early.
  • Parking: On-site parking available but post-event exit is slow. Budget 45+ minutes to leave the lot.
  • Shuttles: Official shuttles from various LA locations. Worth considering to avoid parking headaches.
  • Entry tip: Gates around 4PM but headliners aren't until 10PM+. Arriving 7-8PM often means shorter entry lines.

The Festival Grounds

Four indoor stages, each with its own horror theme and genre focus.

  • Slaughterhouse: The hardstyle/hard dance haven. Intense energy, dedicated crowd, incredible sound system. If you like it fast and heavy, live here.
  • Ghouls' Graveyard: House and techno focus. More underground vibes, groovier energy. Good for when you need a break from intensity.
  • The Chopping Block: Bass music central. Dubstep, riddim, drum & bass. The headbangers congregate here.
  • Cannibals' Tea Party: Main stage energy. Biggest production, most diverse crowd, major headliners. Where casual fans gravitate.

Food, Water & Survival

Indoor venue means no weather worries, but you're still dancing for hours.

  • Temperature: Indoor venues can get hot with packed crowds. Dress in layers or bring something to tie around your waist.
  • Hydration: Water stations available. Lines can be long during peak hours. CamelBaks allowed (empty on entry).
  • Food courts: Between the main stages outdoor area. Halloween-themed options available. Eat early or late to avoid peak lines.
  • Costume repairs: Bring safety pins, tape, backup accessories. Things break when you dance hard.

The Crowd & Community

Halloween brings out a different energy ‚Äî creative, committed, and ready to party.

  • Costume diversity: Everything from elaborate movie characters to sexy cats to full horror makeup. No judgment, only appreciation.
  • Stage personalities: Slaughterhouse has the most intense, dedicated crowd. Main stage is more casual. Find your people.
  • Scare actors: They WILL jump out at you. It's part of the experience. Don't punch anyone (it happens, unfortunately).
  • Photo requests: If your costume is good, expect to be stopped constantly. Factor this into your set schedule.

After the Festival

Getting out and recovering from Escape.

  • Exit strategy: Leave 15 minutes before closing to beat traffic, or stay until the very end and wait out the rush. Middle ground is the worst.
  • Late night food: In-N-Out and Jack in the Box near the venue will be packed. Have a backup plan.
  • Sunday recovery: If you're doing both nights, plan a lazy day between. Or power through ‚Äî your choice.
  • Afterparties: Some clubs in LA do Escape afterparties. Check announcements closer to the event.
