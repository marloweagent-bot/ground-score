Before You Go

Beyond Wonderland is beginner-friendly with minimal prep required.

  • Two days = sustainable: Unlike multi-day camping festivals, you can actually sleep in a bed and recover between days.
  • Study the lineup: Build your schedule but leave room for discovery. Beyond books great undercard acts worth exploring.
  • Plan your costume: The theme encourages creativity. Check the weather (usually 65-75¬∞F) and dress for dancing.
  • Book hotel in Ontario/Rancho Cucamonga: Closer to venue with better options than downtown San Bernardino.

Getting There & Getting In

NOS Events Center is accessible but requires some planning.

  • From LA: About an hour drive. Expect traffic on I-10. Leave early.
  • Shuttles: Insomniac runs shuttles from LA stops. Worth it if you don't want to deal with parking or driving.
  • Parking: On-site parking available. Premier parking worth it for easy exit.
  • Entry: Standard festival security. Arrive early to avoid lines.
  • Re-entry: Yes! You can leave and return, unlike some festivals.

The Festival Grounds

NOS Events Center transforms into a Wonderland with five stages.

  • Queen's Domain (Main Stage): Biggest production, headliners, massive LED walls. Gets crowded for closing sets.
  • Mad Hatter's Castle: Trance and harder styles. Dedicated community with loyal fans.
  • Caterpillar's Garden: House and techno. More intimate vibes, great sound.
  • Cheshire Woods: Bass music. Where the headbangers gather.
  • Looking Glass: Discovery stage for emerging artists.

Food, Water & Survival

March weather makes Beyond one of the more comfortable SoCal festivals.

  • Weather: Expect 65-75¬∞F days, 50-60¬∞F nights. Bring a light jacket for later hours.
  • Water: Free fill stations throughout. Bring a refillable bottle or CamelBak.
  • Food: Standard festival vendors. Eat before peak hours to avoid lines.
  • Comfortable footwear: You'll walk but not as much as massive festivals. Sneakers work fine.

The Crowd & Community

Beyond draws a diverse, welcoming crowd with strong first-timer energy.

  • First-timers welcome: Many people's intro to rave culture. Veterans are generally helpful and friendly.
  • SoCal locals: Heavy local presence means people actually know how to rave.
  • Costume culture: Alice-themed outfits, kandi trading, creative expression encouraged.
  • Age range: Skews 18-28 but all ages present. Less aggressive than pure bass festivals.

After the Festival

Two-day format means easier recovery.

  • Night one recovery: You're sleeping in a hotel, not a tent. Shower, sleep, repeat.
  • After-parties: LA's club scene has events during Beyond weekend. Check Resident Advisor.
  • Sunday exit: Festival ends around 2AM. Plan for traffic or stay overnight and drive Monday.
  • Explore SoCal: If you're visiting, add a beach day or LA exploration before/after.
