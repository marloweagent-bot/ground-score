The Districts

Boomtown is organized into themed districts, each with its own stages, storyline, and atmosphere:

  • Town Centre: The bustling heart ‚Äî shops, street performers, and the main narrative unfolds here.
  • Lions Den: Reggae, dub, and roots music paradise. Massive sound systems and conscious vibes.
  • Sector 6: Dark, industrial techno and the heavier electronic sounds.
  • Paradise Heights: House music haven with disco, funk, and feel-good grooves.
  • Whistlers Green: Folk, acoustic, and world music in a more chilled setting.
  • Relic: Ancient ruins hosting electronic exploration and experimental sounds.

Getting There

  • Coach: Official coaches run from major UK cities. Highly recommended.
  • Driving: Car parks available but expect long queues. Arrive Wednesday to beat the rush.
  • Train: Winchester station, then festival shuttle.
  • Flying: Southampton (30 min) or London Heathrow (1.5 hours).

Camping Options

  • Standard Camping: Included in ticket. Bring your own tent. Some areas are very hilly.
  • Boutique Camping: Pre-pitched tents, tipis, and glamping options available.
  • Campervan: Dedicated fields for vehicles. Book early.
  • Quiet Camping: For those who want to actually sleep. Further from stages.

Survival Tips

  • Footwear: Bring proper boots. The site is hilly and can get muddy. Your ankles will thank you.
  • Costumes: Pack multiple outfits. Fancy dress isn't optional ‚Äî it's essential.
  • Explore: The best stages are hidden. Wander, get lost, follow the sound.
  • Pace yourself: It's five days. Sleep is possible if you plan for it.
  • Cash: Some areas are card-only, others cash-preferred. Bring both.
