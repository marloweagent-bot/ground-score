Before You Go

Das Energi is straightforward to plan ‚Äî no camping logistics, unique but accessible venue.

  • Book Salt Lake City hotels: Downtown SLC is 15 miles from the venue. Hotels are affordable and plentiful.
  • Altitude adjustment: Salt Lake City is at 4,200 feet. If you're from sea level, hydrate extra and take it easier than usual.
  • Dust protection: The salt flats can kick up in dry/windy conditions. Bring a bandana or mask.
  • Layers: August days are warm (80s-90s) but desert nights cool down. Bring a light layer.
  • Sunscreen: High altitude = stronger UV. Protect yourself even on cloudy days.

Getting There & Getting In

Salt Lake City is accessible, but the venue requires transport.

  • Fly into SLC: Salt Lake City International is a Delta hub with good connections. 25-30 minutes to the venue.
  • Car rental or rideshare: The Saltair isn't near public transit. Plan your transport in advance.
  • Parking: On-site parking is available but fills up. Carpool to guarantee a spot.
  • Rideshare strategy: Uber/Lyft work but surge pricing after the fest can be brutal. Pre-plan your exit.
  • Entry is smooth: Boutique scale means no EDC-level lines. Plan 15-20 minutes.

The Festival Grounds

The Great Saltair is a one-of-a-kind venue that makes the festival special.

  • The Saltair building: Historic Moorish-revival architecture from the early 1900s. Indoor and outdoor elements.
  • Main stage: New design in recent years with improved production. Faces the salt flats and mountains.
  • Galactic Flats: Secondary outdoor stage area with unique atmosphere.
  • Synergy Station: Indoor/covered stage option for shade and variety.
  • The setting: Salt flats stretching to the horizon, Great Salt Lake, Wasatch Mountains. Photos don't do it justice.

Food, Water & Survival

August Utah is hot and dry ‚Äî desert survival basics apply.

  • Hydrate constantly: Desert heat plus altitude means you'll dehydrate faster than you expect. Bring a CamelBak.
  • Water stations: Free water available throughout. Use them.
  • Food vendors: Good variety for the size. Utah local vendors add character. Prices are reasonable.
  • Shade: Limited outdoor shade. The indoor areas provide relief. Plan stage hopping around heat.
  • Sunsets: The payoff for the heat ‚Äî sunsets at the Saltair are transcendent. Position yourself at the main stage for golden hour.

The Crowd & Community

The Utah rave scene has its own character ‚Äî passionate and welcoming.

  • Tight community: 15,000 people means real connections. You'll see familiar faces if you're from the region.
  • Welcoming to outsiders: Utah ravers are proud of their scene and happy to share it with visitors.
  • Multi-genre crowd: The lineup diversity brings house heads, bass fans, trance lovers together.
  • Utah culture: Less drinking culture than other festivals (Utah liquor laws + cultural factors). More dancing, less sloppy.
  • PLUR is real: The intimate scale fosters genuine community. People look out for each other.

After the Festival

Salt Lake City has more to offer than you might expect.

  • Downtown SLC: Surprisingly vibrant downtown with good restaurants and bars.
  • Park City: 30 minutes away. Worth a day trip for mountain scenery and boutique vibes.
  • Great Salt Lake: You're right there ‚Äî consider exploring the lake area during daylight.
  • Outdoor activities: Hiking, mountain biking, national parks all within reach. Utah is an outdoor paradise.
  • After-parties: Check local venues ‚Äî V2 Presents often programs after-hours events in SLC.
