Before You Go

Thessaloniki is a destination festival ‚Äî plan for more than just music.

  • Flights: Thessaloniki (SKG) has good European connections. Book early for festival dates.
  • Accommodation: Hotels, hostels, Airbnbs throughout the city. Stay central for easy access.
  • Plan: Multi-venue format means checking schedules and locations each day.
  • Weather: September is perfect ‚Äî warm but not hot. Light layers for evenings.

Getting There & Getting In

City festival means urban infrastructure.

  • Airport: 15km from city center. Buses and taxis available.
  • Venues: Multiple locations across Thessaloniki ‚Äî waterfront, Royal Palace, industrial spaces.
  • Getting around: Walking, taxis, public transport all work.

The Festival Grounds

City venues create diverse experiences across five days.

  • Royal Palace (Kivernio): Historic venue with gardens for main events.
  • Waterfront: Outdoor stages with Aegean Sea backdrop.
  • Clubs: After-parties in Thessaloniki's nightlife spots.
  • Day events: Workshops, talks, screenings in cultural venues.

Food, Water & Survival

Thessaloniki is Greece's food capital. Take advantage.

  • Restaurants: Skip festival food ‚Äî eat at local tavernas. Incredible quality, great prices.
  • Street food: Souvlaki, gyros, bougatsa (sweet pastry) are essential.
  • Bars: Thessaloniki's bar scene is excellent. Pre-party wisely.
  • Pace: September heat is manageable. Stay hydrated.

The Crowd & Community

Greek warmth meets electronic sophistication.

  • Demographics: Greek locals plus growing international attendance.
  • Knowledge: Crowd appreciates innovative programming.
  • Energy: More relaxed than intense rave festivals. Cultural depth.

After the Festival

Stay and explore Greece's most underrated city.

  • History: Roman, Byzantine, Ottoman layers throughout the city.
  • Beaches: Halkidiki peninsula is 1-2 hours away. Perfect post-festival.
  • Nightlife: Thessaloniki's bars and clubs continue the energy.
