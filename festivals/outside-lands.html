Before You Go

Outside Lands is a multi-genre festival first, electronic second. Adjust expectations accordingly.

  • Study the electronic lineup: SOMA and Dolores' are your home bases. Check set times and plan your day around them.
  • SF hotels book fast: August is peak tourist season. Book 2-3 months ahead or pay premium prices.
  • Layer up: San Francisco summer means 60-70¬∞F and potentially foggy. Bring a light jacket ‚Äî you'll need it by evening.
  • No camping: This is a daily festival. You'll need to commute each day, so choose lodging location carefully.

Getting There & Getting In

Golden Gate Park is accessible but SF traffic is real.

  • MUNI is your friend: Extra N-Judah service runs to the park. Takes longer than rideshare but avoids surge pricing.
  • Rideshare surge: End of night surge pricing is brutal. Consider walking to a pickup spot farther from the park.
  • Driving discouraged: Limited parking, expensive, and you won't want to be sober driver after this.
  • Entry: RFID wristband system. Lines worst during first hour of gates opening ‚Äî arrive early or fashionably late.

The Festival Grounds

Know where your people are:

  • SOMA: The dedicated house/techno stage in Marx Meadow. Recently upgraded with better sound and visuals. This is where electronic purists live.
  • Dolores': LGBTQ+ focused dance stage with drag performances and queer artists. More party vibes, less underground.
  • Twin Peaks: Second main stage. Occasionally books electronic-adjacent acts (Jamie xx, Kaytranada) when they're headlining.
  • Other stages: Lands End, Sutro, Panhandle ‚Äî mostly rock, indie, hip-hop. Skip unless you want variety.

Food, Water & Survival

This is OSL's strength ‚Äî embrace it.

  • Eat well: Real SF restaurants operate here. The food is genuinely good, not just "good for a festival."
  • Wine Lands: Curated California wine tastings. Actually educational if you care.
  • Grass Lands: Legal cannabis lounge. Welcome to California.
  • Water: Free refill stations throughout. SF weather means you won't overheat, but stay hydrated.

The Crowd & Community

SF festival crowds are their own thing.

  • Older demographic: More 25-40 than 18-25. Professionals who can afford SF prices.
  • Chill energy: Less raging, more vibing. Don't expect mosh pits or headbanging.
  • SOMA community: House/techno heads who know what they're there for. Quality over quantity crowd.
  • Dolores' community: LGBTQ+ celebration with incredible energy and acceptance.

After the Festival

The party continues if you know where to look.

  • Afterparties: SOMA artists often play late-night sets at SF clubs. Check Audio, Monarch, Public Works.
  • Night scene: SF's house/techno scene is active year-round. Use OSL as an introduction.
  • Recovery: Late-night burritos in the Mission are traditional. Taquerias stay open late.
