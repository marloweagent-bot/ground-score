Before You Go

SMF is straightforward, but Florida in late May requires specific preparation.

  • Book hotels early: Memorial Day weekend fills up Tampa hotels. Downtown or near the stadium is ideal.
  • Hydration pack: Bring a CamelBak or hydration pack. Florida heat is no joke ‚Äî 90¬∞F+ with high humidity.
  • Sunscreen: Seriously. The festival runs into the evening but you'll be in direct sun for hours.
  • Light clothing: This isn't the time for elaborate costumes. Breathable, minimal coverage works best.
  • Check the weather: Memorial Day can bring afternoon thunderstorms. Pack a lightweight rain jacket.

Getting There & Getting In

Raymond James Stadium is a known quantity ‚Äî the infrastructure exists.

  • Fly into TPA: Tampa International is 20 minutes from the venue. Easy flights from most US cities.
  • Driving: Stadium parking is available but fills up. Carpooling helps with cost and exit time.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft work well. Designate a pickup spot away from the main exit to avoid surge madness.
  • Entry lines: Can be long in the heat. Arrive early-ish to avoid peak wait times.
  • Will call: Get your tickets in advance if possible. Will call lines add to the heat exposure.

The Festival Grounds

It's a parking lot with stages. Know what you're walking into.

  • Main stage: Where the headliners play. Biggest crowd, best production, most crowded.
  • Secondary stages: Smaller acts, shorter lines, more room to move. Often the better experience.
  • Shade is limited: Bring a fan, find the misting stations, and rotate between sun and covered areas.
  • Water refill: Free water stations exist but expect lines during peak hours.

Food, Water & Survival

Surviving Florida heat is the real challenge here.

  • Hydrate before you arrive: Start drinking water the day before. You need to be ahead of the dehydration curve.
  • Electrolytes: Pack powder packets or buy sports drinks inside. Water alone won't cut it.
  • Eat early: Get food before sunset when lines are shorter and your stomach is still happy.
  • Know the signs: Dizziness, nausea, headaches = find shade and medical staff immediately.
  • Buddy system: Keep an eye on your group. Heat exhaustion is real and can sneak up on people.

The Crowd & Community

Memorial Day brings out the party crowd.

  • Young and rowdy: 18+ means college-age dominates. Expect high energy and occasional chaos.
  • Florida party culture: This isn't a spiritual gathering ‚Äî it's a party. Adjust expectations accordingly.
  • Mainstream fans: The lineup attracts casual EDM listeners as much as devoted heads.
  • PLUR exists: But it's diluted at this scale. Smaller stages tend to have better vibes.
  • Security presence: Visible and active. Fights get shut down quickly.

After the Festival

Tampa has solid nightlife to extend the weekend.

  • Ybor City: Tampa's nightlife district. Clubs stay open late and attract festival spillover.
  • After-parties: Check local venues for official and unofficial after-parties.
  • Beach recovery: Clearwater and St. Pete beaches are 30-40 minutes away. Perfect for Sunday recovery.
  • Food recovery: Tampa has great Cuban food. Try a Cuban sandwich before you leave.
