Before You Go

III Points is a hotel festival ‚Äî no camping, no shuttles from distant parking lots. Just show up and dance.

  • Tickets: GA starts around $200, tiered pricing rewards early buyers. VIP adds value at III Points (actually better views and amenities).
  • Hotels: Stay in Wynwood for walkability, Miami Beach for the full Miami experience, or Brickell for downtown vibes. All work.
  • When to book: October is shoulder season ‚Äî hotels are available but book a month out for best rates.
  • Weather: October in Miami is warm (80s) and humid. Light clothing, plan for indoor AC breaks.

Getting There & Getting In

Mana Wynwood is in the heart of Miami's art district. No special transport needed.

  • Fly into: Miami International (MIA) is the obvious choice. Fort Lauderdale (FLL) works too if flights are cheaper.
  • Getting around: Uber/Lyft work great. Stay in Wynwood and you can walk to the venue.
  • Entry: Multiple entrances spread crowds. Wristband pickup available early or at gates. Lines move.
  • Re-entry: Typically allowed ‚Äî hit a Wynwood restaurant for dinner and come back for late-night.

The Festival Grounds

Mana Wynwood is a warehouse complex that transforms into a multi-stage festival venue.

  • Main Stage: Outdoor, major headliners, biggest production. Gets crowded for closers.
  • Mind Melt: The techno stage. Indoor warehouse, dark, intense. This is where the purists live.
  • Indoor stages: Air-conditioned relief with quality programming. Don't overlook them.
  • Layout: Compact enough to move between stages easily, spread enough that each feels distinct.

Food, Water & Survival

Urban festival means urban amenities.

  • Food: Quality vendors reflecting Miami's diverse food scene. Cuban, Venezuelan, Asian fusion, American classics. Budget $15-25 per meal.
  • Water: Refill stations throughout. Hydration matters even in October ‚Äî you're dancing in Miami heat.
  • Pro tip: Re-entry allows dinner breaks at Wynwood restaurants. Pace yourself for the late-night sets.
  • Bars: Well-distributed with reasonable lines. Miami drinks mean Miami prices.

The Crowd & Community

III Points attracts music obsessives from Miami and beyond.

  • Who's here: Mix of Miami locals and out-of-towners drawn by the lineup. International crowd reflecting the city.
  • Vibe: Music-focused. People came to discover, not just to be seen. Late-night stages reward commitment.
  • Age: 18+ entry, crowd skews 22-38. Mature but energetic.
  • Dress: Miami casual meets festival style. Comfortable but put-together.

After the Festival

You're in Miami ‚Äî the night doesn't have to end.

  • Afterparties: III Points typically announces official afters. Club Space, Floyd, and other Miami venues host extended programming.
  • Wynwood: Bars and restaurants walking distance from the venue stay open late.
  • Make it a trip: Beach days, South Beach nightlife, Cuban food in Little Havana. Miami rewards exploration.
