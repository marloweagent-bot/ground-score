Before You Go

This is a party, not a festival ‚Äî preparation is minimal.

  • Eat beforehand: Food options inside are limited. Come fed.
  • Halloween costumes: If attending the Halloween edition, commit. The crowd does.
  • Comfortable shoes: You'll be dancing for hours on concrete.
  • Cash and card: Bars take both but have cash as backup.

Getting There & Getting In

DTLA is accessible ‚Äî the challenge is timing.

  • Arrive early: Entry lines have been an issue. Don't arrive at peak time.
  • Rideshare: Works well. Surge pricing on NYE is brutal ‚Äî plan alternatives.
  • Metro: Downtown stations are close. Consider public transit.
  • Parking: DTLA has lots but they fill up. Rideshare is easier.

The Venue

Venues rotate but the aesthetic is consistent ‚Äî industrial DTLA.

  • Multiple rooms: Different sound, different energy. Explore.
  • Main room: Headliners, biggest sound system, most energy.
  • Side rooms: Deeper cuts, more intimate, different genres.
  • Outdoor areas: When available, offer skyline views and fresh air.

Food, Water & Survival

This is a party ‚Äî come prepared.

  • No food vendors: Eat before you arrive. DTLA has options nearby.

  • Water: Available but bars are the priority. Stay hydrated between drinks.

LA's techno community shows up when it matters.

  • Music-focused: People are here to dance, not be seen.
  • Older crowd: Skews late 20s-40s. Experienced ravers.
  • Costume creativity: Halloween editions bring out LA's creative side.
  • Respectful energy: Underground community norms apply.
