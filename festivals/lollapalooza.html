Before You Go

Plan your Perry's Stage priorities and book afterparties in advance.

  • Study the schedule: Perry's runs all day but the closers (10PM) are the big names. Plan backwards from those.
  • Book hotels early: Downtown fills up. River North, South Loop, or West Loop put you near transit and nightlife.
  • Afterparty tickets: Official Lolla aftershows at clubs like Radius and Prysm sell out. Buy in advance.
  • Transit pass: Get a CTA pass for the L trains. You won't want to Uber in festival traffic.

Getting There & Getting In

Grant Park is downtown Chicago. Access is easy but crowds are real.

  • By train: Red, Blue, Orange, Green lines all have nearby stops. This is the way.
  • By rideshare: Works for getting there. Leaving is a nightmare ‚Äî walk 10 blocks then call an Uber.
  • By hotel: If you're staying downtown, walk. Park closes at 10 PM; you'll be out by 10:30.
  • Entry gates: Multiple entrances ‚Äî check which is closest to Perry's and use that one.

The Festival Grounds

Grant Park spans 319 acres. Know where to go.

  • Perry's Stage: Your home base. Located at the south end of the park. Massive LED setup, dedicated EDM programming all day. Closers are can't-miss.
  • The Grove: Secondary electronic stage with more indie/experimental electronic. Four Tet, Nia Archives type bookings.
  • Main stages: Worth visiting for crossover acts or breaks from Perry's. The skyline views are worth it.
  • Pro tip: Perry's gets packed by 8 PM. Either commit to a spot early or embrace the back.

Food, Water & Survival

Chicago summers are humid. The festival is long. Pace yourself.

  • Hydrate: Bring an empty water bottle. Refill stations exist but lines form in afternoon heat.
  • Eat real food: Chow Town has quality options ‚Äî Big Star tacos, Lou Malnati's pizza. Worth the price.
  • Afternoon breaks: Perry's afternoon sets are less crowded. Use this time for food, shade, and recharging.
  • Weather prep: August storms roll in. Check forecast. Ponchos > umbrellas (umbrellas block views).

The Crowd & Community

Perry's draws a younger, rowdier crowd. Adjust expectations.

  • Mosh pits: They happen. Front-center is chaos. If that's not your scene, stay sides or back.
  • Crowd density: Headliners pack the stage. Arrive 1-2 acts early for good spots.
  • Mixed audience: Not everyone is a dedicated raver. You'll meet casual fans alongside die-hards.
  • Cell service: Unreliable with 100K people. Make meeting plans in advance.

After the Festival (The Real Show Begins)

The 10 PM curfew is a feature, not a bug. Chicago's nightlife takes over.

  • Official aftershows: Radius, Prysm, Soundbar host artists who just played Lolla. Intimate club sets after festival shows.
  • Chicago house scene: The birthplace of house music. Smartbar and Spybar for underground sets any night.
  • Book in advance: Popular aftershows sell out. Don't assume you'll walk up.
  • Pace yourself: Festival + afterparty = 4-5 AM nights. You're doing this for 4 days. Sleep matters.
