Before You Go

Lost Lands is a camping festival in rural Ohio. You need to plan accordingly.

  • Camping is mandatory: There are no hotels nearby. You're sleeping at Legend Valley or driving 45+ minutes each day (don't do this).
  • Car camping vs tent camping: Car camping is worth the extra cost‚Äîaccess to your vehicle for supplies and AC naps.
  • Bring earplugs: 1,000,000+ watts of bass will damage your hearing. High-fidelity earplugs are essential, not optional.
  • Prepare for weather: September in Ohio can be hot days, cold nights, muddy, or dusty. Pack layers and rain gear.
  • Neck exercises: Start them now. Three days of headbanging adds up.

Getting There & Getting In

Legend Valley is remote. Plan your logistics early.

  • Drive: Most people drive. It's about an hour east of Columbus, Ohio.
  • Fly + rent car: Columbus (CMH) is your airport. You'll need a rental car‚Äîno rideshare or shuttle makes sense from there.
  • Early arrival: Wednesday early arrival passes let you set up camp before crowds arrive. Worth it for good spots.
  • Entry search: They check cars and bags. Know the prohibited items list.

The Festival Grounds

Legend Valley is a natural amphitheater transformed into prehistoric bass paradise.

  • Prehistoric Paradox (Main Stage): Excision's touring rig scaled way up. Massive LED screens, more lasers than you can count. All three Excision headlining sets happen here.
  • Wompy Woods: Shaded forest stage with deep dubstep. Popular during hot afternoon hours.
  • Raptor Valley: Secondary stage with stacked lineups. Less crowded than main but still huge sound.
  • Fossil Falls: Smaller stage for discovery acts and b2b sets.
  • The dinosaurs: They're everywhere. Animatronic T-Rex, fossils, prehistoric environments. The theming is committed.

Food, Water & Survival

You're camping for four days. Plan your supplies.

  • Bring food for camp: Coolers, easy meals, snacks. You don't want to buy every meal inside.
  • Water: Free fill stations exist but bring bottles. Stay hydrated‚Äîbass music is physical.
  • Festival food: Standard vendors inside. Spicy Pie is the crowd favorite. Prices are typical festival markup.
  • Hearing protection: This is survival gear. The sound pressure at Paradox stage can cause permanent damage. Use earplugs.
  • Dust/mud prep: Bandanas, boots, and a good attitude. Ohio weather is unpredictable.

The Crowd & Community

Lost Lands has one of the most dedicated communities in electronic music.

  • Headbanger family: These people are here for bass music specifically. Everyone knows the same artists, the same songs, the same drops.
  • Mosh pits: They happen. They're intense. But there's an unwritten code‚Äîpick people up, make space for those who need out.
  • Camp neighbors: You'll become friends. The camping community is tight‚Äîsharing food, trading kandi, staying up until dawn together.
  • All experience levels: First-timers are welcomed and guided by veterans. The community teaches its own.

After the Festival

Lost Lands aftercare is real.

  • Your ears: They'll be ringing. Give them a break. See an audiologist if it persists.
  • Your neck: Three days of headbanging takes a toll. Stretch, ice, rest.
  • The drive home: Most people leave Monday. It's a long drive after four days of camping. Get sleep first.
  • Post-festival depression: It's real after Lost Lands. The community feeling is hard to leave. Stay connected on Reddit, Discord.
