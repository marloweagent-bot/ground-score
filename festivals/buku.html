Before You Go

BUKU is a city festival, so preparation is different from camping events.

  • Book hotels early: French Quarter and Warehouse District are closest. Prices spike during festival weekend.
  • Plan your NOLA experience: Build in time for the city. Come early or stay late.
  • Check the weather: March in NOLA can be perfect or rainy. Layers and rain gear recommended.
  • Comfortable shoes: You'll be on concrete all day, and walking back to hotels at night.

Getting There & Getting In

One of the most accessible festivals in America thanks to New Orleans' infrastructure.

  • Fly into MSY: Major airport, cheap flights, 20-30 min from downtown.
  • Stay downtown: French Quarter and CBD are walking distance to venue.
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft work great. Surge pricing after headliners.
  • Entry: Will call and ticket scanning fairly efficient. Arrive before headliners to avoid lines.

The Festival Grounds

Mardi Gras World provides an industrial playground right on the Mississippi.

  • Main Stage: Outdoor, river-adjacent. Where the biggest acts play.
  • Power Plant: Indoor warehouse stage with heavy bass. Gets LOUD.
  • Ballroom: More intimate indoor space for hip-hop and experimental.
  • Float Den: Unique stage built into Mardi Gras float warehouse.

Food, Water & Survival

  • Food is legitimately good: This is New Orleans. Expect quality local vendors.
  • Hydrate: March can be warm. Water stations available but bring a bottle.
  • Pace yourself: New Orleans nightlife will tempt you. Festival + late nights = brutal.

The Crowd & Community

  • College crowd: Lots of LSU, Tulane, and regional university students.
  • Music heads: People who appreciate genre diversity.
  • NOLA locals: The city shows up for its festival.
